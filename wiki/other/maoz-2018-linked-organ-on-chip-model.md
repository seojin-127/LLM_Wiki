## Summary
Three coupled microfluidic organ-on-chip devices (two BBB chips flanking a brain chip) model the complete human neurovascular unit (NVU). The system dissects cell-type-specific contributions to NVU function and reveals previously unknown metabolic coupling between BBB endothelium and neurons. Models intravascular methamphetamine administration and CNS effects.

## Key Contributions
- First linked multi-chip NVU: influx BBB chip → brain chip → efflux BBB chip
- Primary human cells: brain endothelium, pericytes, astrocytes (BBB) + mixed neural cells (brain)
- Dissects individual cell-type contributions to NVU homeostasis
- Discovers endothelial-neuronal metabolic coupling (novel finding)
- Models methamphetamine pharmacokinetics and CNS effects in vitro

## Methods & Architecture
- Three coupled microfluidic chips with continuous medium flow
- BBB chips: hBMVECs + pericytes/astrocytes on semipermeable membrane
- Brain chip: hippocampus-derived neural stem cells → ~60% glia, 40% neurons (glutamatergic, GABAergic, dopaminergic, serotonergic)
- Mass spectrometry metabolomics for metabolite tracking across chips

## Results
- BBB chip: tight junction formation, selective permeability demonstrated
- Novel metabolic coupling: BBB endothelium produces metabolites that influence neuronal function
- Methamphetamine crosses BBB, alters neuronal metabolic state in downstream chip
- Individual cell-type contributions to NVU resolved by selective chip omission

## Limitations
- Primary human cells; iPSC-derived NVU not implemented
- Simplified geometry; single-path flow vs. in vivo 3D complexity
- Metabolite identification limited by mass spec sensitivity
