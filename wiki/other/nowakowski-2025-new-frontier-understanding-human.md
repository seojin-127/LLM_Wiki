## Summary
Nature Perspective co-authored by major brain atlas and developmental neuroscience groups (Nowakowski, Bhaduri, Zeng, Kriegstein, Linnarsson, Sestan, etc.). Synthesizes recent advances in comprehensive cell atlases of the developing human brain and model organisms, highlights novel developmental cell populations, conserved and divergent cross-species patterns, and links these to neurodevelopmental and neuropsychiatric disorder mechanisms.

## Key Contributions
- Synthesizes current state of brain developmental atlases (human, mouse, NHP)
- Highlights novel developmental cell populations discovered via single-cell approaches
- Reviews conserved and divergent patterns of cell genesis, migration, and maturation across species
- Connects transcriptional control of cell fate to complex behaviors and NDD risk
- Identifies future directions: adolescence, whole-brain single-cell, cross-species integration

## Methods & Architecture
- Perspective/review — no primary experimental data

## Results
- Summary of cell atlas achievements and their implications for NDD understanding
- Emphasis on linking cell-type resolution to disorder mechanisms
- Future: prenatal→adolescence time courses; whole-brain single-cell; cross-species integration

## Limitations
- Perspective only; rapidly evolving field
