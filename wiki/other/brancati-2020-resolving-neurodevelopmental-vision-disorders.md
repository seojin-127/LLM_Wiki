## Summary
Perspective article reviewing how single-cell multi-omics tools characterize brain and retina organoid fidelity and limitations, and how organoids can model congenital brain malformations and vision disorders. Compares organoids to animal model limitations and proposes protocol improvements based on single-cell readouts.

## Key Contributions
- Reviews single-cell multi-omics (scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq) applied to brain and retinal organoids
- Identifies organoid artifacts and limitations detected by single-cell tools (e.g., stress signatures)
- Discusses applications to congenital brain malformations (microcephaly, lissencephaly) and retinal disorders
- Compares organoid to animal models: mouse lacks fovea; marmoset has near-lissencephalic brain
- Proposes using single-cell data to guide organoid protocol optimization

## Methods & Architecture
- Perspective/review — no primary experimental data

## Results
- Single-cell multi-omics reveals organoid-specific transcriptional artifacts and incomplete patterning
- Multi-omics cell type matching: organoid cells can be mapped to in vivo counterparts
- Retinal organoids complement brain organoid systems for CNS disease modeling

## Limitations
- Perspective article — no experimental validation
- Field has advanced substantially since 2020
