## Summary
Landmark paper establishing the cerebral organoid technology. hPSC-derived 3D brain organoids self-organize into discrete brain regions with oRG-like progenitors characteristic of human cortex. Patient iPSC model of CDK5RAP2 microcephaly shows premature differentiation explaining reduced brain size.

## Key Contributions
- First hPSC-derived cerebral organoid system (landmark, 2013)
- Self-organized regions: cortex, retinal pigment, choroid plexus-like structures
- oRG-like cells present; human OSVZ-like progenitor zone organization
- Primary microcephaly model: CDK5RAP2 patient iPSC + RNAi → premature differentiation

## Methods & Architecture
- hPSC in Matrigel → spinning bioreactor; no directed patterning
- Histology, immunofluorescence for brain region characterization

## Results
- VZ/SVZ/cortical plate-like regions with inside-out layering
- oRG-like cells in OSVZ position (absent in mice)
- Microcephaly organoids: premature differentiation → reduced progenitor pool

## Limitations
- Unguided → heterogeneous and variable between batches/experiments
- No vascularization → necrotic core in large organoids
- No reproducible regional identity (addressed by Pasca 2015 and subsequent protocols)
