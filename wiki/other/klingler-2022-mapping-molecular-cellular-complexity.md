## Summary
Science review mapping molecular and cellular mechanisms underlying cortical malformations (microcephaly, lissencephaly, polymicrogyria, focal cortical dysplasia, heterotopias). Integrates human genetics with progenitor biology, mouse models, and organoid findings to explain how disrupted developmental processes produce specific structural cortical abnormalities.

## Key Contributions
- Comprehensive classification and mechanistic review of cortical malformations
- Links specific genetic mutations to disrupted developmental processes (proliferation, migration, lamination)
- Centrosome dysfunction → microcephaly; actin/tubulin → lissencephaly; mTOR → focal dysplasia
- Convergence of diverse mutations on shared cellular vulnerability points
- Integrates organoid and mouse model findings with human genetic data

## Methods & Architecture
- Review article — no primary experimental data

## Results
- Malformation types map onto distinct developmental vulnerabilities:
  - Microcephaly: progenitor under-proliferation (centrosome genes)
  - Lissencephaly: neuronal migration defects (LIS1, DCX, actin/tubulin)
  - Polymicrogyria: post-mitotic lamination defects
  - Focal dysplasia: mTOR pathway dysregulation
  - Heterotopia: migration arrest

## Limitations
- Review only; no experimental data
