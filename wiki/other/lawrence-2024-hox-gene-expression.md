## Summary
First single-cell + spatial transcriptomics atlas of the developing human fetal spine, revealing that neural crest derivatives retain the HOX code of their anatomical origin (HOX "source code") while also adopting the destination code. Distinct dorsal/ventral HOX patterns in spinal cord mapped; HOXB genes lose collinearity in dorsal domains.

## Key Contributions
- HOX "source code": neural crest derivatives carry dual HOX identity (origin + destination)
- Trend confirmed across multiple organs
- Ventral spinal cord: motor pool HOX organization
- HOXB collinearity loss in dorsal spinal cord domains

## Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq + spatial transcriptomics + in situ sequencing of human fetal spine

## Results
- Neural crest derivatives: retain origin HOX code; adopt destination HOX code
- Multi-organ validation; motor pool mapping; HOXB loss of collinearity

## Limitations
- Fetal tissue only; mechanism of HOX retention not elucidated
