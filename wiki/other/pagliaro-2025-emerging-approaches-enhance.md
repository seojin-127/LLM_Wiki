## Summary
Review of emerging strategies to improve brain organoid physiological relevance: stem cell quality, morphogen optimization, ECM manipulation, assembloid fusion, co-cultures, and in vivo xenotransplantation. Synthesizes current approaches and speculates on future paths toward more realistic brain models.

## Key Contributions
- Reviews stem cell optimization and morphogen strategies for improved organoid reproducibility
- Surveys ECM and tissue architecture manipulation approaches
- Covers assembloids, co-cultures, and in vivo xenotransplantation for cellular diversity
- Outlines future directions for physiologically advanced organoids

## Methods & Architecture
Review article (Trends in Cell Biology).

## Results
- Assembloids (fused region-specific organoids) improve cellular diversity and circuit formation
- In vivo xenotransplantation provides vascularization and maturation signals
- ECM and morphogen optimization improve lamination and batch-to-batch reproducibility

## Limitations
Review; primary experimental validation is in cited literature.
