## Summary
Nature Protocols paper describing generation of human telencephalic brain organoids from single isolated neural rosettes. Single-rosette origin produces more reproducible cellular composition and predictable spatial organization vs. multi-rosette organoids, with functionally mature neurons. Includes CRISPR-Cas9 SHANK3 deletion protocol for ASD disease modeling.

## Key Contributions
- Single neural rosette-derived organoids: consistent cellular composition, predictable lumen-centered organization
- Mimics singular neural tube origin in vivo (unlike multi-rosette protocols)
- Functionally mature neurons: action potentials, excitatory + inhibitory synaptic inputs
- CRISPR-Cas9 hemizygous SHANK3 deletion as ASD/Phelan-McDermid syndrome model
- Complete characterization pipeline: scRNA-seq + immunohistochemistry + patch-clamp electrophysiology

## Methods & Architecture
- CRISPR-Cas9 engineering of iPSCs for SHANK3 hemizygous deletion
- Isolation of single neural rosettes from stem cell cultures
- Telencephalic patterning from single rosette → organoid
- Slice patch-clamp electrophysiology for functional characterization
- scRNA-seq for cellular composition profiling
- Protocol duration: 5–6 months

## Results
- Single rosette-derived organoids: reproducible organization around single lumen
- Functional neural networks validated electrophysiologically
- SHANK3 deletion organoids model synaptic pathology
- Applications noted: biocomputers, disease modeling

## Limitations
- Long protocol (5–6 months); requires cell culture expertise
- Single rosette isolation labor-intensive
- Scalability for large screens limited by manual steps
