## Summary
First single-cell long-read isoform atlas of mouse and human brain: 72% of genes show full-length isoform variation across cell types, regions, developmental stages, or species. Cell-type-specific splicing is strongest. Adolescent transition (P21→P28) shows peak developmental isoform regulation. Mouse-to-human conservation maintained in hippocampus; human has additional gain-of-function isoforms.

## Key Contributions
- 72% of genes isoform-variable across ≥1 axis (cell type, region, development, species)
- Splicing, TSS, polyA: all strongly cell-type-specific
- Adolescent (P21→P28) = strongest developmental splicing window
- Mouse→human: cell-type-specific isoforms mostly conserved; human has gain-of-function additions
- Disease variants linked to cell-type-specific isoforms

## Methods & Architecture
- Single-cell long-read sequencing (PacBio Iso-Seq / ONT) across brain regions, timepoints
- Human hippocampus cross-species comparison; developmental trajectory

## Results
- 72% isoform variable; cell type > region regulation
- Neurotransmitter transport + synapse genes: cell-type isoform variation across regions
- P21→P28: strongest developmental isoform switch

## Limitations
- Long-read per-cell coverage lower; rare isoforms may be missed
- Human limited to hippocampus
