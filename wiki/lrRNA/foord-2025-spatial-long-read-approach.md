## Summary
Spl-ISO-Seq: spatial long-read sequencing at near-single-cell resolution in human visual cortex. Pre- vs. post-puberty comparison shows layer 4 excitatory neurons have the most developmentally regulated splicing changes; cortex has stronger splicing regulation than white matter; changes linked to post-synaptic function.

## Key Contributions
- Spl-ISO-Seq: spatial long-read method with 2-3× longer reads and near-single-cell resolution
- Spl-ISOquant: custom spatial long-read analysis software
- Layer 4 excitatory neurons: most pubertal splicing regulation across cortical layers
- Cortex > white matter for splicing/polyA regulation; oligodendrocytes stronger in WM

## Methods & Architecture
- PacBio long-read sequencing on spatially barcoded cDNA (near-single-cell resolution)
- Human post-mortem visual cortex: pre-puberty (8–11 y) vs. post-puberty (16–19 y)

## Results
- Layer 4 excitatory neurons: highest developmentally regulated splicing changes
- Repeat elements downstream of developmentally regulated layer 4 exons
- Alternative splicing changes linked to post-synaptic structure/function genes

## Limitations
- Post-mortem tissue; RNA quality variability
- Near-single-cell (not true single-cell) resolution
- Only two age groups; continuous trajectory not captured
