## Summary
~1.2M single-cell transcriptomes from young adult and aged mice (both sexes, brain-wide); 847 cell clusters, 14 age-biased (mostly glial), 2,449 age-DE genes. Identifies the hypothalamic third-ventricle region (tanycytes, ependymal, arcuate/DMH/PVN neurons) as the primary aging hub.

## Key Contributions
- Brain-wide aging atlas: ~1.2M cells, forebrain/midbrain/hindbrain, both sexes
- 847 cell clusters; 14 age-biased clusters (mostly glial)
- 2,449 unique age-DE genes across neuronal and non-neuronal types
- Third ventricle hypothalamus: dual decrease in neuronal function + increase in inflammation = aging hub

## Methods & Architecture
- snRNA-seq of brain regions from young adult + aged mice (both sexes)
- 847-cluster high-resolution cell type taxonomy; age-DE gene analysis

## Results
- Common aging signature: ↓neuronal structure/function genes in neurons, astrocytes, oligodendrocytes; ↑immune genes in microglia/vascular cells
- 14 age-biased clusters = mostly glial (some enriched, some depleted with age)
- Third ventricle tanycytes/ependymal/hypothalamic neurons: highest aging sensitivity, dual dysfunction

## Limitations
- Mouse only; human equivalent atlas lacking
- Cross-sectional design; no longitudinal tracking
- Mechanisms of aging not experimentally validated
