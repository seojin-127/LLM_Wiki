## Summary
Jourdon et al. used forebrain organoids from 13 father-son pairs (ASD vs. unaffected) to model idiopathic ASD. Macrocephalic and normocephalic ASD probands show opposite imbalances of excitatory cortical neuron subtypes, driven by TF divergence during early neurogenesis. No single genomic variant explains findings, but rare-variant ASD genes overlap with altered transcripts — convergence between idiopathic and rare-variant ASD.

## Key Contributions
- First idiopathic ASD organoid study using father-son pairs (n=13 families)
- Macrocephalic vs. normocephalic ASD: opposite E/I neuron subtype imbalances
- TF expression divergence drives lineage imbalance
- Convergence: rare-variant ASD genes overlap idiopathic ASD transcriptome

## Methods & Architecture
- Forebrain organoids from ASD probands vs. unaffected fathers
- scRNA-seq; cell type composition; rare variant enrichment analysis

## Results
- Macrocephalic: excess dorsal CP; normocephalic: excess preplate-like
- TF divergence explains lineage imbalance; no single explanatory genomic variant
- ASD rare-variant gene set enriched in altered transcripts

## Limitations
- 13 families: modest sample size for heterogeneous condition; father as control shares 50% genetics
