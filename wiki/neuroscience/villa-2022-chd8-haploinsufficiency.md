## Summary
CHD8 haploinsufficiency in cerebral organoids causes macrocephaly-like expansion, accelerates inhibitory neuron production, delays excitatory neuron production (E/I imbalance), and disrupts mRNA splicing in post-mitotic neurons. Effects on radial glia proliferation are temporally restricted and cell-type-specific.

## Key Contributions
- Macrocephaly-like: CHD8 mutant organoids larger
- Accelerated GABAergic + delayed excitatory neuron production (E/I imbalance)
- Temporally restricted radial glia proliferation changes
- mRNA processing dysregulation in post-mitotic neurons

## Methods & Architecture
- CRISPR CHD8 haploinsufficiency; cerebral organoids; scRNA-seq time course + size measurement + splicing analysis

## Results
- Earlier GABAergic; later excitatory; macrocephaly-like; aberrant splicing in neurons

## Limitations
- No functional circuit readout; haploinsufficiency only
