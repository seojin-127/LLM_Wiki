## Summary
ARID1B haploinsufficiency impairs corpus callosum development in a callosal organoid model. ARID1B+/- reduces chromatin accessibility at TF binding sites and dysregulates transcriptional programs in SATB2+ neurons, impairing maturation and long-range axon formation.

## Key Contributions
- Corpus callosum organoid model (SATB2+ callosal projection neurons)
- ARID1B+/- → reduced chromatin accessibility → transcriptional dysregulation of CC programs
- Maturation and long-range axon formation impaired
- Mechanistic link: ARID1B → chromatin → CC development → agenesis

## Methods & Architecture
iPSC-derived SATB2+ callosal neurons; ARID1B+/- isogenic lines; scRNA-seq + snATAC-seq; axon tracing; fetal brain comparison.

## Results
- Chromatin accessibility reduced at specific TF binding sites in ARID1B+/-
- CC transcriptional programs downregulated
- Impaired neuronal maturation and axon projection

## Limitations
Organoid limitations; corpus callosum agenesis is multifactorial.
