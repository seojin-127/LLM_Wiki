## Summary
Patient-derived forebrain organoids carrying homozygous CNTNAP2 truncating mutations recapitulate cortical overgrowth via increased neural progenitor proliferation. scRNA-seq identifies PFC excitatory neurons as key CNTNAP2-expressing cells. CRISPR correction of the mutation rescues organoid overgrowth.

## Key Contributions
- Forebrain organoid model of CNTNAP2-associated ASD with clinical brain overgrowth phenotype
- Increased organoid volume and cell number driven by progenitor over-proliferation
- PFC excitatory neurons identified as primary CNTNAP2-expressing cell type
- DEGs enriched for ASD risk genes; CRISPR correction rescues phenotype

## Methods & Architecture
- hiPSC from CNTNAP2 homozygous mutation patients + forebrain organoid differentiation
- Volumetric analysis + scRNA-seq for DEG and cell type characterization
- CRISPR-Cas9 correction as rescue experiment

## Results
- Patient organoids: increased volume and cell number vs. isogenic controls
- Neural progenitor over-proliferation drives overgrowth
- PFC excitatory neurons have highest CNTNAP2 expression
- DEGs enriched for ASD risk variants and CNTNAP2 patient clinical features
- Mutation correction → normalized organoid size

## Limitations
- Rare syndromic ASD (homozygous truncating); unclear generalizability to idiopathic ASD
- Organoid lacks full cortical architecture and connectivity
- Mechanism of CNTNAP2 loss → progenitor over-proliferation not fully established
