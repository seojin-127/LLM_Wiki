## Summary
iPSC-derived NSCs from idiopathic ASD individuals show pathological priming: premature activation of late developmental gene networks. This heterochronicity is a convergent phenotype across ASD individuals, implicating specific fetal cortical windows as ASD-vulnerable.

## Key Contributions
- Pathological priming: late gene networks activated too early in ASD NSCs
- Heterochronicity is convergent across idiopathic ASD (not single-gene)
- Time-series transcriptomics: iPSC → NSC → neuron differentiation
- Maps ASD vulnerability to specific fetal cortical development stages

## Methods & Architecture
iPSC from idiopathic ASD + controls; NSC/neuron time-series RNA-seq; WGCNA gene network analysis; fetal brain dataset comparison.

## Results
- Convergent priming phenotype across multiple ASD individuals
- Temporal alignment shows premature network activation in ASD NSCs

## Limitations
Bulk RNA-seq; iPSC model limitations; idiopathic ASD heterogeneity.
