## Summary
Review proposing a framework for linking rare protein-disrupting variants in profound autism to cell-type function using brain transcriptomic atlases and foundation models. Addresses the challenge of broad gene expression patterns versus cell-type-specific vulnerability. Proposes combining experimental perturbations, AI-driven inference, and postmortem validation as a unifying mechanistic framework.

## Key Contributions
- Framework quantifying trade-off between a gene's cell-type specificity and sensitivity in ASD
- Reviews how brain transcriptomic atlases decode cellular consequences of rare gene-disrupting variants
- Proposes foundation models (perturbation prediction) to clarify variant → cell-type → phenotype links
- Identifies limited overlap between genetic associations and transcriptomic alterations in autistic brains
- Unifying framework: experimental perturbation + AI inference + postmortem validation

## Methods & Architecture
- Review/perspective article; no primary experimental data

## Results
- Broad ASD gene expression confounds cell-type-specific effects → need for specificity × sensitivity framework
- Foundation model perturbation prediction could clarify variant → cellular function links
- Postmortem brain validation essential to confirm AI-driven predictions

## Limitations
- Review/framework paper; experimental validation pending
- Foundation model rare-variant predictions limited by training data distribution
