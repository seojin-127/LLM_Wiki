## Summary
Three top ASD risk genes (KMT5B, ARID1B, CHD8) were knocked to haploinsufficiency in human cortical organoids (745K+ cells). All three converge on asynchronous development of GABAergic vs. deep-layer excitatory neurons despite acting through distinct molecular pathways. Individual genomic context modulates phenotype expressivity. Calcium imaging confirms downstream abnormal circuit activity.

## Key Contributions
- Convergence: 3 diverse ASD genes → same cell-type vulnerability (GABAergic + deep-layer excitatory timing)
- Distinct molecular pathways per gene; convergence at cellular level
- Genomic context modulates expressivity (gene × background interaction)
- Calcium imaging: early developmental defects → abnormal circuit activity

## Methods & Architecture
- hCS organoids with CRISPR haploinsufficiency; multiple donor lines per gene
- 745K+ scRNA-seq cells; proteomics; GCaMP calcium imaging

## Results
- All 3 mutations: GABAergic accelerated + deep-layer excitatory delayed (asynchronous)
- Donor-specific expressivity variation
- Downstream circuit abnormalities in GCaMP imaging

## Limitations
- Organoid model; limited circuit complexity; haploinsufficiency only
