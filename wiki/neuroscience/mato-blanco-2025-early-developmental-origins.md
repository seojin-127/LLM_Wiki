## Summary
Risk gene expression dynamics across human corticogenesis identify critical vulnerability phases for ASD/NDD in NSCs. GRN simulation of TF depletion reveals spatiotemporal-dependent effects. ASD patient NSCs show recurrent TF alterations in brain patterning and lineage commitment programs.

## Key Contributions
- Risk genes enriched in brain organizers and sequential GRN programs throughout corticogenesis
- Disease-specific critical phases when NSCs are most vulnerable to gene dysfunction
- GRN-based TF depletion simulation: spatiotemporal-dependent trajectory effects
- ASD patient NSC scRNA-seq: recurrent TF alterations in patterning and lineage commitment

## Methods & Architecture
- Human NSC expression datasets (in vitro + in vivo) across telencephalic fate transitions
- ASD/ID/SCZ/epilepsy risk gene expression dynamics; GRN simulation of TF depletions
- scRNA-seq of ASD patient iPSC-derived NSCs

## Results
- Brain organizers = hubs of cortical and NDD risk gene expression
- ASD/NDD have disease-specific critical phases of NSC vulnerability
- TF depletion shows spatiotemporal-specific effects on cortical cell trajectories
- ASD patient NSCs: recurrent TF expression alterations in brain patterning genes

## Limitations
- In vitro NSC models; in vivo validation needed
- TF depletion is computational simulation; not experimental perturbation
