## Summary
Chronic GC exposure in unguided human neural organoids amplifies inhibitory neuron cell fate via PBX3 TF, with lasting cell-type-specific transcriptional changes overlapping ASD risk genes. Demonstrates that prenatal GC exposure (an environmental risk factor) and ASD genetic variants may converge on the same lineage specification pathway (E/I balance disruption).

## Key Contributions
- GC → biased inhibitory neuron fate via PBX3 (identified by scRNA-seq + scATAC-seq)
- Lasting ASD risk gene dysregulation from GC exposure
- Convergence: genetic + environmental risk via shared lineage specification mechanism
- GRN characterization of GC-responsive transcriptional changes

## Methods & Architecture
- Chronic dexamethasone treatment in unguided hPSC neural organoids
- scRNA-seq + scATAC-seq integrated for GRN analysis
- Comparison to GC-free controls; ASD risk gene overlap

## Results
- Inhibitory neuron lineage amplified (PBX3 priming)
- Cell-type-specific lasting transcriptional changes including ASD risk genes
- GC-responsive TFs linked to chromatin accessibility changes in inhibitory progenitors

## Limitations
- Unguided organoid lacks spatial/regional resolution
- Pharmacological vs. endogenous GC; dose/timing unclear
