## Summary
DM1 patient-derived cortical organoids show CUG foci → CELF2 hyperphosphorylation → MECP2 pathway gene dysregulation in glutamatergic neurons. Convergent defects with MECP2-null organoids link DM1 to ASD-related molecular mechanisms. NMDA antagonists rescue neuronal defects.

## Key Contributions
- DM1 → CUG foci → CELF2 dysfunction → MECP2 pathway misregulation in glutamatergic neurons (scRNA-seq + eCLIP)
- Convergent phenotypes between DM1 and Rett syndrome (MECP2-null) organoids
- NMDA antagonists as therapeutic candidate for DM1 neuronal defects

## Methods & Architecture
3D cortical organoids from DM1 patient iPSCs; scRNA-seq; eCLIP for CELF2 binding; MECP2(y/-) organoid comparison; glutamate toxicity assays.

## Results
- CUG foci → CELF2 hyperphosphorylation → reduced RNA binding
- MECP2 pathway genes misregulated specifically in glutamatergic neurons
- Glutamate toxicity and neuronal loss converge with Rett syndrome model

## Limitations
Single iPSC line; no microglia/vasculature; in vivo validation needed.
