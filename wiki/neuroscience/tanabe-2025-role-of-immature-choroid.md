## Summary
ChP-specific CAMDI knockout mice exhibit immature choroid plexus and ASD-like behaviors including a delayed social critical period. ASD patient iPSC-derived organoids recapitulate the immature ChP phenotype, validating the mouse model in human cells. Early postnatal metformin (P0–P21) rescues both ChP maturation and social behavior.

## Key Contributions
- ChP immaturity established as ASD pathological feature (mouse + human iPSC convergence)
- Delayed social critical period mechanistically linked to immature ChP
- Metformin as potential early intervention: rescues ChP maturation and behavior
- iPSC organoid validation of mouse phenotype (ChP gene expression signature)

## Methods & Architecture
- ChP-specific CAMDI-KO mice; behavioral + histological phenotyping
- ASD patient iPSC → choroid plexus organoids; transcriptomic comparison to CAMDI-KO
- Metformin treatment P0–P21; behavioral and molecular readouts

## Results
- CAMDI-KO: immature ChP, ASD-like behaviors, delayed social critical period
- ASD iPSC organoids: immature ChP signature matching mouse model
- Metformin: improved ChP maturation markers, normalized social behavior, no critical period delay

## Limitations
- Single ASD risk gene (CAMDI); broader ASD heterogeneity unclear
- Organoid lacks complete ChP architecture and CSF secretion function
