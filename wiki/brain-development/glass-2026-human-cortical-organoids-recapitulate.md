## Summary
iPSC-derived human cortical organoids from individuals with paired longitudinal infant brain MRI data demonstrate that early in vitro fate decisions correlate with in vivo cortical surface area. Organoid cell-type proportions, growth rate, and cell cycle gene expression in early progenitors capture inter-individual variability in brain growth trajectories.

## Key Contributions
- Cortical organoids derived from participants with longitudinal infant brain imaging (IBIS Network)
- Early cell-type proportions (including hem and choroid plexus) correlate with infant cortical surface area in vivo
- Cell cycle genes in early neural progenitors associated with inter-individual growth variability
- Validates organoids as a system that faithfully reflects individual genetic background effects on brain development

## Methods & Architecture
- iPSC-derived cortical organoids from genetically diverse participants
- Paired longitudinal infant brain MRI data (Infant Brain Imaging Study Network)
- scRNA-seq for cell-type quantification; organoid size measurements

## Results
- Organoid cross-sectional area growth rate correlates with infant cortical surface area
- Hem and choroid plexus proportions associated with individual brain growth variability
- Cell cycle gene expression in early neural progenitors reflects genetic background differences

## Limitations
- Organoid model: limited laminar and areal cortical identity resolution
- Correlation-based study; causal mechanisms between organoid and in vivo phenotypes require follow-up
- Infant MRI only; later developmental correspondence not assessed
