## Summary
Seminal review covering the cell biology of cortical neural stem/progenitor cells: their classification (NE → RG → oRG/IP), subcellular features (cilia, adherens junctions, apical/basal processes), division modes, and cross-species differences. Establishes the conceptual framework for understanding how outer radial glia (oRG) cells drive neocortex expansion in gyrencephalic species.

## Key Contributions
- Classification framework: NE → RG → oRG (OSVZ) and IPs
- oRG cells as key evolutionary innovation for gyrified neocortex expansion
- Cell polarity, cilia, and basal process as determinants of progenitor vs. differentiation fate
- INM linked mechanistically to cell cycle phase

## Methods & Architecture
- Review synthesis; covers molecular genetics, live imaging, and comparative anatomy literature up to 2014

## Results
- oRG enriched in human/ferret (gyrencephalic) vs. absent/rare in mouse (lissencephalic)
- Asymmetric vs. symmetric divisions controlled by adherens junction inheritance, cilia, and basal process
- Extracellular signals (Notch, FGF, Wnt) regulate NE→RG transition and progenitor pool maintenance

## Limitations
- Pre-single-cell-era review; transcriptomic heterogeneity within progenitor types not captured
