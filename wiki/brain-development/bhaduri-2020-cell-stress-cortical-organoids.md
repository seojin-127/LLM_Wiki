## Summary
scRNA-seq comparison of primary human fetal cortex vs. organoids shows organoids lack molecular subtypes and areal specification due to ectopic cellular stress pathway activation. Transplantation into mouse cortex alleviates stress and partially rescues subtype specification.

## Key Contributions
- Organoids have broad cell classes but lack molecular subtypes and areal specification
- Ectopic stress pathways (UPR, oxidative stress) impair cell-type specification in organoids
- Transplantation into mouse cortex: reduces stress, partially rescues molecular subtype defects
- Framework for evaluating organoid accuracy using primary tissue as reference

## Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq of primary human fetal cortex + organoid cells; transplantation into mouse cortex
- Comparison across developmental periods and cortical areas

## Results
- Primary cortex: diverse progenitor/neuron subtypes + areal molecular signatures
- Organoids: broad classes present, molecular subtypes absent, areal signatures not spatially organized
- Stress genes (UPR) ectopically activated in organoids
- Transplantation → reduced stress → partial subtype recovery

## Limitations
- Primary tissue from limited gestational ages; transplantation uses immunocompromised mice
- Stress mechanism not fully resolved at molecular level
