## Summary
Human-macaque comparative single-cell atlas (snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq + spatial) of postnatal PFC development. Human-specific prolonged maturation driven by higher glial progenitor proliferation. Identifies key synaptogenesis, pruning, and gliogenesis windows, and cell types most vulnerable to neurodevelopmental disorders.

## Key Contributions
- Paired snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq + spatial transcriptomics of human and macaque postnatal PFC
- Human-specific prolonged PFC maturation; glial progenitors more proliferative than macaque
- Key developmental windows for synaptogenesis, synaptic pruning, and gliogenesis
- NDD/psychiatric disorder risk enriched in specific cell types and lineages
- Human-specific TFs regulating extended maturation programs

## Methods & Architecture
- 38 human neocortical samples + matched macaque samples; first trimester to adolescence
- Paired multiome (snRNA + snATAC) + Visium spatial; cross-species trajectory comparison

## Results
- Human glial progenitors: higher proliferation capability than macaque → extended maturation
- Synaptogenesis/pruning: key postnatal windows identified
- Neurodevelopmental risk genes enriched in specific cell types
- Human-specific TF expression features identified

## Limitations
- Postnatal focus; prenatal not covered
- Macaque sample matching may not be perfectly equivalent
