## Summary
Single-cell CUT&Tag profiling of H3K27ac, H3K27me3, and H3K4me3 across human neural organoid differentiation from pluripotency to retinal and brain progenitors. H3K27me3 deposition at the neuroectoderm stage is functionally required for downstream fate restriction — EZH2 inhibition at this stage disrupts retinal vs. brain identity.

## Key Contributions
- First single-cell multi-histone atlas across human neural organoid differentiation trajectory
- Epigenomic state changes precede transcriptional identity shifts
- H3K27me3 at neuroectoderm stage functionally required for fate restriction (EZH2 inhibition experiment)
- Bivalent domains (H3K4me3+H3K27me3) resolve during neural commitment

## Methods & Architecture
- Single-cell CUT&Tag for H3K27ac, H3K27me3, H3K4me3
- Multiple organoid protocols (retinal + brain specification)
- Trajectory analysis from iPSC → neuroepithelium → lineage-specified progenitors
- Functional perturbation: EZH2 inhibitor at neuroectoderm stage

## Results
- Epigenomic transitions precede transcriptional differentiation
- H3K27me3 marks genes to be silenced before transcriptional silencing occurs
- EZH2 inhibition at NE stage → disrupted retinal/brain fate separation
- Bivalent domain resolution is a key step in neural lineage commitment

## Limitations
- Organoid chromatin dynamics may not fully recapitulate in vivo trajectories
- Single-cell CUT&Tag has lower coverage than bulk; rare states may be missed
- Only three histone marks profiled
