## Summary
Gabriel et al. demonstrated that iPSC-derived brain organoids can self-organize bilateral symmetric optic vesicles (OVB-organoids) from the forebrain-like region without artificial patterning signals. OVB-organoids contain retinal cell types (photoreceptors, RPE, RGC-like) and exhibit functional light sensitivity — enabling inter-organ brain-retina interaction within a single organoid.

## Key Contributions
- Self-organized bilateral optic vesicles in brain organoids (~15–30% efficiency)
- Neural + non-neural retinal cell types; functional light response
- Platform for brain-retina inter-organ studies

## Methods & Architecture
- Unguided iPSC organoids; IHC + scRNA-seq; calcium imaging; 3D imaging

## Results
- Bilaterally symmetric optic vesicles from week 4–5
- Retinal cell types confirmed; photosensitivity demonstrated

## Limitations
- Low/variable efficiency; immature cell types; no vascularization
