## Summary
HARE5, a HAR enhancer of Frizzled8/WNT signaling, drives human-specific cortical expansion. Human HARE5 knock-in mice have enlarged neocortices with more excitatory neurons. Four human-specific HARE5 variants increase progenitor proliferation via WNT amplification in human and chimp organoids, showing how small regulatory changes drive major evolutionary differences.

## Key Contributions
- Hs-HARE5 knock-in mice: enlarged neocortex, more excitatory neurons, increased cortical functional independence
- Mechanism: increased radial glia self-renewal → expanded neurogenic potential
- Four human-specific variants drive increased enhancer activity in NPCs/organoids
- WNT signaling amplification is the molecular mechanism

## Methods & Architecture
Genome-edited mouse knock-in; scRNA-seq; live imaging + lineage tracing; human/chimp NPC + organoid editing; in vivo cortical independence measurements.

## Results
- Human HARE5 enlarges neocortex in mouse via WNT
- Human-specific HARE5 variants identified as functional drivers

## Limitations
Mouse model limitations; one HAR of thousands; combinatorial effects unknown.
