## Summary
Prospective lineage tracing of 6,402 human cortical progenitors reveals the lineage map of human cortical development. Progenitors switch from glutamatergic to GABAergic output at midgestation (coinciding with OPC onset). Truncated RG maintain glutamatergic potential protractedly; their late-born neurons show deep-layer molecular features.

## Key Contributions
- First lineage-resolved map of human cortical development: 6,402 progenitors traced
- Glutamatergic→GABAergic neurogenesis switch at midgestation; coincides with OPC onset
- Truncated RG (tRG): protracted glutamatergic neurogenic potential
- Late-born tRG-derived neurons: deep-layer molecular features + subplate contribution

## Methods & Architecture
- Lentiviral clonal barcoding of progenitors in primary human cortical tissue
- 6,402 progenitors; scRNA-seq readout per clone; neurogenesis + gliogenesis window

## Results
- Midgestation: glutamatergic→GABAergic switch; OPC genesis onset
- tRG: continue generating glutamatergic neurons beyond the switch
- Late-born tRG neurons: deep-layer (not upper-layer) molecular identity; subplate expansion

## Limitations
- Ex vivo lineage tracing window; in vivo validation needed
- Rare progenitors may be missed
