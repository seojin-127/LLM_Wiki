## Summary
Mansour et al. established an in vivo model by transplanting GFP+ hPSC-derived cerebral organoids into the adult NOD-SCID mouse brain. Grafted organoids become vascularized by the host, undergo progressive neuronal maturation over 8+ months, extend axons to host brain regions, and form functional synaptic connections confirmed by two-photon Ca2+ imaging and optogenetics.

## Key Contributions
- First in vivo vascularized human brain organoid model via mouse brain engraftment
- Functional vasculature (authentic host blood microenvironment) — overcomes necrotic core limitation
- Graft-to-host synaptic connectivity demonstrated with optogenetics
- Host microglia integration into grafts

## Methods & Architecture
- hESC → GFP+ cerebral organoid (40–50 days) → intracerebral implantation in retrosplenial cortex
- Immunodeficient NOD-SCID host; longitudinal IHC up to 233 dpi
- In vivo two-photon microscopy (GCaMP, FITC-dextran); optogenetics (ChR2 + extracellular recording)

## Results
- 92% host survival, 80% graft integration (n=28), grafts persist to 233 dpi
- Progressive NPC→neuron maturation; axonal projections to corpus callosum, thalamus
- Spontaneous Ca2+ activity and functional blood flow confirmed in vivo
- Optogenetic graft stimulation → host neuron responses (functional synaptic connectivity)

## Limitations
- Immunodeficient mouse required; no immune modeling
- Forebrain-dominant (PAX6+/CTIP2+); limited regional diversity
- Chimeric circuitry; not fully human
