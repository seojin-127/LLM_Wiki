## Summary
First forebrain assembloid: fusion of pallial hCS and subpallial hSS spheroids models saltatory GABAergic interneuron migration and functional circuit integration. Timothy syndrome (CACNA1C) interneurons show migration defects rescuable by roscovitine.

## Key Contributions
- First assembloid system fusing dorsal (hCS) and ventral (hSS) forebrain spheroids
- Models human interneuron saltatory migration in vitro for the first time
- Functional GABAergic integration into hCS microcircuits demonstrated electrophysiologically
- Timothy syndrome model: migration defect + partial rescue with roscovitine

## Methods & Architecture
- hSS differentiated via WNT/SHH modulation; NKX2-1+, GABAergic
- Physical fusion of hCS + hSS as assembloid; live-imaging of migration
- scRNA-seq (~11,838 cells), patch clamp and MEA electrophysiology

## Results
- Saltatory interneuron migration from hSS into hCS captured by live imaging
- Interneurons functionally integrate: inhibitory postsynaptic currents in hCS neurons
- GABAergic subtypes: SST, calretinin, calbindin; parvalbumin emerges after 200 days
- Timothy syndrome hSS: reduced saltatory migration, partial rescue with roscovitine

## Limitations
- Physical juxtaposition fusion; may not fully recapitulate in vivo migration signals
- No vascularization or thalamic inputs
- Long maturation period required for full interneuron subtype specification
