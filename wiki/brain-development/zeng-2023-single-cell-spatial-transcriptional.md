## Summary
Single-cell RNA-seq and spatial transcriptomics atlas of human gastrulation and early neural development (Carnegie stages 7–13). Captures the neuroepithelium-to-radial-glia transition and spatially resolved regional progenitor specification, revealing human-specific transcriptional features absent in mouse.

## Key Contributions
- First single-cell + spatial atlas of human gastrulation through early neural tube formation
- Traces NE→RG transformation with intermediate states at single-cell resolution
- Spatially distinct RG subtypes along anterior-posterior and dorsal-ventral axes
- Human-specific transcriptional signatures in early neural progenitors

## Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq of Carnegie stage 7–13 human embryos
- Spatial transcriptomics (10x Visium) for regional validation
- Cross-species comparison with mouse gastrulation datasets
- Trajectory analysis from NE through early RG specification

## Results
- Continuous NE→RG transition captured with transient intermediate states
- Forebrain vs. hindbrain vs. spinal progenitors show distinct TF programs
- WNT, FGF, SHH signaling shows region-specific gradients
- Human-specific genes enriched in early neural progenitors

## Limitations
- Rare embryonic tissue limits cell numbers per Carnegie stage
- 10x Visium spatial resolution insufficient for fine anatomical mapping
- Human cross-species comparison limited to available mouse datasets
