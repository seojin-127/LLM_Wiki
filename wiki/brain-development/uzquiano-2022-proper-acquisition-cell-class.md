## Summary
Multi-modal atlas (scRNA + ATAC + spatial) of human cortical organoid development. Cell class identity acquisition matches endogenous cortex and is not substantially distorted by metabolic state. Pseudo-time trajectories predict human-specific fate specification genes; callosal neuron diversity emerges early.

## Key Contributions
- scRNA-seq + single-cell ATAC-seq + spatial atlas of hCS organoids across development
- Organoid cell class identity closely matches endogenous cortex
- Metabolic stress does not substantially alter cell class identity
- Pseudo-time analysis predicts human-specific regulatory genes
- Callosal neuron transcriptional diversity detectable at early stages

## Methods & Architecture
- Multi-modal single-cell profiling at multiple organoid developmental stages
- Primary human cortex comparison for fidelity assessment

## Results
- Cell class identity: organoid ≈ endogenous cortex
- Metabolic state does not bias major cell class assignments
- Human-specific fate specification genes identified via pseudo-time prediction
- Callosal neuron diversity: emerges early in organoid development

## Limitations
- Cell subtype (beyond class) specification may still be impaired
- Human-specific predictions require in vivo validation
