## Summary
Pasca et al. introduced the human cortical spheroid (hCS) — a simple, matrix-free 3D cortical organoid from hPSCs. hCS contains deep (CTIP2+) and superficial (SATB2+) layer neurons transcriptionally matching fetal cortex (~19–24 weeks), with electrophysiologically mature and synaptically connected neurons plus nonreactive astrocytes. Foundational protocol for hCS-based disease modeling.

## Key Contributions
- Original hCS protocol: reproducible, matrix-free, no Matrigel
- Both deep and superficial layer cortical neurons; nonreactive astrocytes
- Transcriptional match to fetal human cortex
- Functional synapses and network activity in acute slices

## Methods & Architecture
- Directed differentiation (no Matrigel); self-organizing in ultra-low attachment
- Bulk RNA-seq; patch-clamp; calcium imaging; IHC

## Results
- CTIP2+ and SATB2+ neurons; transcriptional match to ~19–24 GW fetal cortex
- Mature action potentials, spontaneous synaptic events, network bursting
- Astrocytes: GFAP+, nonreactive

## Limitations
- No vascularization; no inhibitory interneurons; early stage only
