## Summary
Paired scRNA-seq + scATAC-seq atlas of hypothalamic preoptic area (POA) across postnatal development. Identifies four developmental stages; sex differences emerge perinatally; vomeronasal sensory input is required for normal timing of cell type maturation. Trajectories are shaped by sex, location, and cell function.

## Key Contributions
- Four stages of POA development: early diversification → perinatal sex differences → postnatal maturation → weaning/puberty
- Sex shapes trajectories before puberty (perinatal emergence)
- Vomeronasal sensing required for timing of preoptic maturation (sensory mutant evidence)
- Cell type function and anatomical location predict developmental trajectory shape

## Methods & Architecture
Paired scRNA-seq + scATAC-seq across postnatal time points; male/female comparison; vomeronasal mutant mice.

## Results
- Nonlinear transcriptional changes accelerate at weaning and puberty
- Chromatin accessibility dynamics resolve regulatory changes per cell type
- Sex differences in chromatin/RNA present before puberty

## Limitations
Mouse model only; spatial resolution limited; functional validation needed.
