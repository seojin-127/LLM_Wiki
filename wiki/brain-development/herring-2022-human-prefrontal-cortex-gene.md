## Summary
snRNA-seq and snATAC-seq atlas of human prefrontal cortex from gestation to adulthood. Documents major gene expression reconfiguration at the prenatal-postnatal transition and continuous maturation into adulthood. Key finding: long-term brain organoids lack postnatal-mature neurons even after years in culture.

## Key Contributions
- Full developmental arc of human PFC gene regulation (gestation → adulthood) at single-cell resolution
- Cell-type-specific maturation timing: diverse, protracted across cell types
- Identifies regulatory networks predicting cellular and temporal origins of brain disease
- Organoid benchmark: long-term organoids have few postnatal-mature neurons

## Methods & Architecture
snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq across PFC developmental time points; trajectory analysis; TF regulon inference; integration with organoid datasets.

## Results
- Prenatal-to-postnatal transition: largest single gene expression reconfiguration event
- Continuous reconfiguration continues throughout adulthood
- Disease-relevant regulatory networks mapped to developmental trajectories

## Limitations
Tissue quality variation; limited intermediate postnatal time points; snRNA-seq cytoplasmic transcript gap.
