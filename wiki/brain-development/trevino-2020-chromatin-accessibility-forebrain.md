## Summary
20-month ATAC-seq + RNA-seq time course of dorsal/ventral forebrain organoids shows chromatin accessibility matching in vivo human brain. Maps disease risk variants (ASD, SCZ, epilepsy) onto cell-type-specific open chromatin, identifying specific lineages and developmental windows as disease-susceptible.

## Key Contributions
- Organoid chromatin accessibility recapitulates in vivo patterns over 20 months
- Dynamic enhancer landscape: progenitor → neuron → glia
- Disease risk variants (ASD/SCZ/epilepsy) enriched in cell-type-specific open chromatin
- Identifies cell type + developmental window of increased disease susceptibility

## Methods & Architecture
3D dorsal/ventral forebrain organoids; ATAC-seq + RNA-seq over 20 months; comparison to primary brain tissue; disease risk enrichment analysis.

## Results
- Chromatin dynamics match in vivo across time
- Putative lineage-specific enhancers identified
- Cell-type-resolved disease risk mapping

## Limitations
No microglia/vasculature; original bulk ATAC-seq (limited cell-type resolution).
