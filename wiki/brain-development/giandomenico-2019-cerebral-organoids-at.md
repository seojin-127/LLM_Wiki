## Summary
Giandomenico et al. grew cerebral organoids at an air-liquid interface (ALI-COs), dramatically improving cell survival and enabling extensive axon outgrowths that form nerve tracts. Live imaging shows authentic axon guidance dynamics; MEA recordings demonstrate spontaneous functional neuronal activity. Scalable in vitro method requiring no transplantation.

## Key Contributions
- ALI culture: greatly improved organoid survival (reduced necrotic core)
- mm-scale nerve tract formation with directed axon outgrowths
- Live imaging: authentic growth cone and fasciculation dynamics
- MEA: spontaneous activity; pharmacologically responsive

## Methods & Architecture
- Cerebral organoids on ALI membrane inserts; live two-photon imaging + MEA

## Results
- Improved survival; extensive nerve tract formation
- Axon guidance dynamics match in vivo; MEA burst activity recorded

## Limitations
- No vascularization; still diffusion-limited for large organoids
