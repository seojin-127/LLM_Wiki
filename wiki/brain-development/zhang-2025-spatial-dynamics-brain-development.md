## Summary
Spatial tri-omic atlas (ATAC+RNA+protein and H3K27me3+RNA+protein from same tissue section) of mouse brain P0-P21 using DBiT technology + CODEX imaging. Reveals temporal persistence of layer-defining TF chromatin accessibility, dynamic myelin gene priming in corpus callosum, and molecular programs shared between brain development and neuroinflammation.

## Key Contributions
- First spatial tri-omic platform (DBiT) enabling simultaneous ATAC/CUT&Tag + RNA + protein from same section
- P0-P21 spatiotemporal atlas + human second-trimester brain comparison
- Layer-defining TF chromatin accessibility persists and spreads spatially postnatally
- Corpus callosum: chromatin priming of myelin genes; projection neurons coordinate axonogenesis
- Neuroinflammation and developmental programs overlap at molecular level

## Methods & Architecture
- Spatial ARP-seq (ATAC+RNA+protein) and CTRP-seq (CUT&Tag+RNA+protein) via DBiT
- 23-plex CODEX for mouse, 19-plex for human; ~10 µm spatial resolution
- P0-P21 mouse developmental time series + lysolecithin neuroinflammation model

## Results
- Layer-defining TF (cortex) chromatin accessibility spreads spatially over P0-P21
- Corpus callosum myelin genes: chromatin primed before myelination onset
- Projection neurons coordinate axonogenesis and myelination across CC subregions
- Neuroinflammation microglia: distal activation + shared developmental gene programs

## Limitations
- DBiT resolution ~10 µm (near but not single-cell); human data limited to second trimester
- LPC neuroinflammation model may not fully recapitulate human demyelinating disease
