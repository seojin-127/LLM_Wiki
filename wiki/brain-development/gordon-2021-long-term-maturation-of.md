## Summary
Human cortical spheroids (hCS) cultured over 20 months undergo intrinsically programmed maturation matching key early postnatal transitions. Transcriptomic profiling shows progression from neurogenesis to astrogenesis aligning with in vivo postnatal human brain, demonstrating that organoids can model beyond mid-fetal stages.

## Key Contributions
- hCS maturation timeline mirrors early postnatal human brain development (neurogenesis → astrogenesis)
- Intrinsic programs drive maturation timing, not extrinsic signals
- Systematic transcriptomic and functional benchmarking against in vivo postnatal brain
- Foundation for modeling neurodevelopmental and neuropsychiatric disorders at postnatal stages

## Methods & Architecture
Directed hCS differentiation from pluripotent stem cells; scRNA-seq time series (up to 20+ months); comparison to human fetal/postnatal brain datasets; functional network activity analysis.

## Results
- Transcriptomic alignment with early postnatal in vivo stages achieved at late time points
- Intrinsic astrogenesis timing conserved across iPSC lines
- Functional maturation (network activity) observed at late culture stages

## Limitations
No vasculature/microglia; iPSC line variability; full postnatal maturation not achievable.
