## Summary
Paired snATAC + snRNA-seq atlas of 38 human neocortical samples (first trimester to adolescence, PFC + V1) with spatial transcriptomics and lineage tracing. Discovers Tri-IPCs: tripotential progenitors generating GABAergic neurons, OPCs, and astrocytes. ASD GWAS risk enriched in second-trimester intratelencephalic neurons. Glioblastoma cells resemble Tri-IPCs.

## Key Contributions
- 38 human neocortical samples: paired snATAC + snRNA across first trimester to adolescence
- Tri-IPCs: novel tripotential intermediate progenitors (GABAergic + OPC + astrocyte)
- ASD risk: second-trimester intratelencephalic neurons most enriched
- Glioblastoma transcriptome resembles Tri-IPCs (developmental hijacking hypothesis)
- Cell-type/age/area-specific GRNs characterized

## Methods & Architecture
- Paired multiome (snATAC + snRNA); 38 PFC + V1 samples; spatial transcriptomics
- Progenitor purification + lineage tracing experiments

## Results
- Tri-IPCs at neurogenesis-gliogenesis transition; tripotential lineage output
- ASD risk genes enriched in second-trimester intratelencephalic (IT) neurons
- Glioblastoma correlates with Tri-IPC transcriptome

## Limitations
- 38 samples across long developmental span; some stages underrepresented
