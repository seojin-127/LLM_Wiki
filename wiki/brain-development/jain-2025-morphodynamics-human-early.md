## Summary
Jain et al. established long-term live light-sheet microscopy of fluorescently labelled brain organoids (multi-mosaic, multi-protein), quantifying tissue morphology and cell behaviors during neuroepithelial induction, lumenization, and regionalization over weeks. Extrinsic matrix drives lumen expansion and telencephalon formation via WNT/YAP1 mechanosensing; absence of matrix pushes organoids toward neural crest and caudalized identity.

## Key Contributions
- First long-term live light-sheet morphodynamics platform for brain organoids
- Quantitative morphometric atlas of organoid development (actin, tubulin, nucleus, membrane)
- Extrinsic matrix (Matrigel) essential for telencephalon formation via mechanosensing
- WLS: earliest marker of non-telencephalic region emergence
- WNT/YAP1: mechanosensing pathway linking matrix to regional specification

## Methods & Architecture
- Multi-mosaic hiPSC lines; light-sheet microscopy; computational demultiplexing
- scRNA-seq integrated with morphometric data; ±Matrigel comparison

## Results
- Matrix-free: neural crest + caudalized fate; lumen expansion reduced
- Lumenization linked to ECM pathway and mechanosensing gene programs
- WNT and Hippo (YAP1) mediate matrix-induced telencephalon specification

## Limitations
- Unguided organoid variability; specialized imaging equipment needed
