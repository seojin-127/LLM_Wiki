## Summary
Comprehensive snRNA-seq atlas of mouse telencephalic GABAergic neurons (611K adult + 614K developmental cells) establishes a four-level taxonomy: 7 classes, 52 subclasses, 284 supertypes, 1,051 clusters. Reveals that long-distance migration is common across many subtypes and that cortical/striatal diversification occurs primarily postnatally.

## Key Contributions
- Adult taxonomy: 7 classes, 52 subclasses, 284 supertypes, 1,051 clusters (611K cells)
- Developmental taxonomy: 1,688 clusters (614K cells) tracking progenitor-to-mature progression
- Long-distance migration is widespread, not exceptional
- Cortical and striatal subtypes diversify postnatally (P7–P21 window)

## Methods & Architecture
- snRNA-seq of adult mouse telencephalon + developmental time series
- Spatial transcriptomics for anatomical localization
- Hierarchical taxonomy: class → subclass → supertype → cluster
- Developmental trajectory analysis

## Results
- 7 adult classes encompass full telencephalic GABAergic diversity
- 1,051 terminal clusters at finest resolution
- Developmental dataset: 1,688 clusters; diversification is progressive not saltatory
- Long-distance migration: multiple subclasses originate far from final position
- Postnatal transcriptomic diversification: peaks P7–P21 for cortical and striatal types

## Limitations
- Mouse only; human equivalent taxonomy lacking
- Spatial resolution limits fine mapping within small nuclei
- Developmental pseudotime inference, not live single-cell tracking
