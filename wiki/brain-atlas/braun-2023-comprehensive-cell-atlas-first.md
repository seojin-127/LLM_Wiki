## Summary
BICCN first-trimester human brain atlas: 1.67M cells from 26 specimens (5–14 pcw), 616 clusters. Reveals three concurrent neocortical programs, IPC balance, MGE→thalamus GABAergic migration, regional glioblast diversity, and early OPC regionalization. First comprehensive whole-brain first-trimester map.

## Key Contributions
- 1,665,937 cells from 111 samples (26 specimens, 5–14 pcw); 616 clusters
- Spatial transcriptomics at 5 pcw (FISH + Visium)
- Three concurrent neocortical programs: differentiation, cell cycle, maturation
- CRABP+ MGE cells predicted to become adult thalamic PVALB+ neurons
- Regional glioblast diversity → mechanism for adult region-specific astrocyte types

## Methods & Architecture
- sci-RNA-seq3 on 111 biological samples from 26 first-trimester specimens
- Multiplexed RNA FISH + Visium for spatial validation at 5 pcw
- 616 clusters annotated with class, subclass, spatial location, and age

## Results
- Neocortical excitatory lineage: differentiation/cell cycle/maturation programs run concurrently
- IPCs: neurogenic TF induction after G1 phase; delicate progenitor-neurogenic balance
- MGE CRABP+ GABAergic migration to thalamus → future PVALB+ thalamic interneurons
- Ventral midbrain: diverse progenitors at 8 pcw; TH subtypes emerge after 14 pcw
- Regional glioblasts → region-specific astrocyte types
- OPCs: forebrain/midbrain/hindbrain-specific with conserved functional gene expression

## Limitations
- First trimester only; second trimester and beyond covered by other atlases
- Visium spatial resolution (55 µm spots) limits fine-grained spatial annotation
- Rare human embryonic tissue limits per-stage cell counts
