## Summary
Brain-wide transcriptomic taxonomy of 65,002 mouse spinal projecting neurons (SPNs) from 32 brain regions identifies 76 region-specific types in three functional components. A LIM homeobox TF code parcellates reticulospinal neurons into 5 molecular populations.

## Key Contributions
- 65,002 SPNs, 76 region-specific types; first brain-wide SPN transcriptomic taxonomy
- Three-component SPN organization: excitatory point-to-point (cortex/cerebellum), heterogeneous reticular (global modulation), modulatory neuropeptide (gain-setting)
- LIM homeobox code: 5 distinct reticulospinal neuron populations
- Large-soma transcriptional signatures correlate with fast-firing electrophysiology

## Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq of retrogradely labeled SPNs (32 source regions, 24 spinal targets)
- 225 source-to-target combinations; integrated with Allen Brain Cell Atlas

## Results
- Cortical/cerebellar SPNs: homogeneous excitatory; somatotopic spinal terminations
- Reticular formation SPNs: heterogeneous; broad termination patterns
- Modulatory SPNs: hypothalamus/midbrain/hindbrain; neuropeptides for gain-setting
- LIM homeobox TF code → 5 molecularly and spatially segregated reticulospinal populations

## Limitations
- Mouse only; human SPN taxonomy lacking
- Retrograde labeling may miss low-abundance SPNs
