## Summary
Brain Cell Atlas: 26.3M cells integrated from 70 human + 103 mouse studies (multiple developmental stages, all brain regions). ML-based consensus annotation. Discovers PCDH9-high microglia subpopulation with region-specific regulatory differences between hippocampus and PFC.

## Key Contributions
- 26.3M cells from 173 studies — largest integrative brain atlas
- ML consensus cell type annotation across diverse datasets
- Putative neural progenitor cells identified in adult human brain
- PCDH9-high microglia subpopulation; hippocampus vs. PFC regulatory divergence

## Methods & Architecture
- Integration of 70 human + 103 mouse studies across developmental stages and brain regions
- ML-based consensus annotation; cell-cell communication network analysis

## Results
- Largest integrative human+mouse brain atlas (26.3M cells)
- ML annotation resolves cross-dataset label inconsistencies
- PCDH9-high microglia: distinct GRNs in hippocampus vs. PFC

## Limitations
- Integration artifacts across 173 diverse-protocol datasets
- PCDH9-high microglia functional role not experimentally validated
