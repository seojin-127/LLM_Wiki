## Summary
Epi-retro-seq links single-cell epigenomes and cell types to long-distance projections for 33,034 mouse neurons across 32 source regions to 24 targets (225 combinations). Integrated into the BICCN atlas; spatial transcriptomics assigns projection clusters to sub-source regions.

## Key Contributions
- epi-retro-seq: retrograde tracing + single-cell epigenomics; 33,034 neurons
- 225 source-to-target projection combinations; 926 statistical discriminability comparisons
- Epigenomic identity predicts projection target
- Integration into BICCN atlas linking projection types to consensus clusters

## Methods & Architecture
- Retrograde viral injection + single-cell methylome/chromatin profiling (epi-retro-seq)
- 32 brain source regions, 24 projection targets; spatial transcriptomics for sub-regional mapping

## Results
- Epigenomic cell types are predictive of long-distance projection identity
- Detailed analysis of hypothalamus, thalamus, hindbrain, amygdala, midbrain projections
- Sub-source region resolution via spatial transcriptomics integration
- 926 pairwise statistical comparisons establish discriminability atlas

## Limitations
- Mouse only; human projection epigenomics pending
- Retrograde viral efficiency varies by target region
