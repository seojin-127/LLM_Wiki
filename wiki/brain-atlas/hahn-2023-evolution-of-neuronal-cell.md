## Summary
Cross-species single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the retina from 17 vertebrate species (humans to lamprey). The six retinal cell classes are highly conserved across vertebrates; variation increases from outer retina (photoreceptors) to inner retina (RGCs). Mouse orthologues of primate midget RGCs are identified, suggesting these cells are evolutionarily ancient rather than primate innovations.

## Key Contributions
- Integrated scRNA-seq/snRNA-seq from 17 species; transcriptomic variation scales with evolutionary distance
- 6 cell classes conserved; subclass conservation high; cell type variation pronounced within subclasses
- Evolution preferentially shapes retinal output (RGCs diverge more than photoreceptors)
- Mouse midget RGC orthologues identified: ~2% of mouse RGCs, large receptive fields; both primate and mouse project to thalamus → V1

## Methods & Architecture
Multi-species scRNA-seq/snRNA-seq integration; cross-species cell type matching by conserved gene expression programs; phylogenetic analysis of transcriptomic variation.

## Results
- All 6 retinal cell classes molecularly conserved across 17 vertebrate species
- Numerous cell types shared across species, tracing to early ancestral vertebrate
- Midget RGCs >80% of human RGCs; mouse orthologues ~2% with large receptive fields
- Overrepresentation of midget RGC orthologues in thalamus in both primates and mice

## Limitations
- Rare cell types may be underrepresented; functional validation of cross-species homologies needed
