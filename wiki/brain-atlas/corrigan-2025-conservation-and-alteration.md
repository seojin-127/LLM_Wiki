## Summary
Cross-species scRNA-seq across 10 mammalian species (160 Myr) shows that TAC3 striatal interneurons — previously considered primate-specific — are in fact an ancestral MGE-derived population conserved across placental mammals. In mice, the homologous population is masked by reduced Tac2 and gained Th expression. Cell type evolution acts via redistribution and fate refinement, not novel precursors.

## Key Contributions
- TAC3 initial class is ancestral and conserved across Eutheria (pig, ferret, mouse), not primate-specific
- Mouse TAC3 homologue = Th striatal interneurons; rare Tac2 subpopulation in ventromedial striatum
- Initial inhibitory neuron classes conserved; terminal class diversity arises via fate refinement
- Cross-species framework: initial class → extrinsic refinement → terminal class

## Methods & Architecture
scRNA-seq (10x) of developing cortex/striatum in 10 species; Harmony integration; RNAscope spatial validation in pig/ferret/porpoise; adult striatum comparison.

## Results
- Inhibitory neuron initial classes segregate by cell type, not species (Pearson correlation)
- TAC3/CRABP1/LHX6 population confirmed in striatum of pig, ferret, porpoise
- Mouse targeted MGE enrichment reveals conserved Tac2 subpopulation in ventromedial striatum

## Limitations
Functional differences between TAC3/Th homologues unknown; tissue availability limited for some species.
