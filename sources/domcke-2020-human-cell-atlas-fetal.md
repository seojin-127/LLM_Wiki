## One-line Summary
sci-ATAC-seq3 chromatin accessibility atlas from ~800K human fetal cells (15 organs, 59 samples, 89–125 days PCW); reveals TF regulators of cell fate; endothelial cells organ-specific; blood cells conserved across organs; companion to Cao et al. 2020.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Science
- Published: 2020

## 2. Key Contributions
- sci-ATAC-seq3: improved combinatorial-indexing ATAC-seq (3 rounds); lower cost, no custom reagents
- ~800K single-cell chromatin accessibility profiles from 59 fetal samples (15 organs, 89–125 PCW)
- Cell type annotation via parallel gene expression data (Cao et al. 2020)
- TF motif analysis: known and novel regulators of cell fate specification/maintenance
- TFs classified as activators or repressors based on expression-accessibility correlation
- Organ-specific endothelial chromatin; blood cell types similar across organs

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- sci-ATAC-seq3: three rounds of combinatorial indexing for scalable single-cell ATAC-seq
- 59 human fetal samples, 15 organs, 89–125 days post-conceptional age
- Cell type annotation via overlapping gene expression data from Cao et al. 2020
- TF motif enrichment analysis per cell type; activator/repressor classification

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- ~800K cells; 15 organs = comprehensive fetal chromatin atlas
- TF motifs in accessible sites explain cell type affiliation
- Activator TFs: expression and motif accessibility positively correlated
- Repressor TFs: expression and motif accessibility negatively correlated
- Endothelial cells: highly organ-specific chromatin accessibility
- Blood cell types: chromatin largely conserved across organs

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- 89–125 days PCW window; earlier and later stages not covered
- Chromatin accessibility alone cannot determine transcriptional activity
- Requires paired gene expression for cell type annotation

## 6. Related Work
- Cao et al. 2020: companion human fetal expression atlas (same Science issue)
- Mannens et al. 2025: first-trimester chromatin accessibility in brain (different window)

## 7. Glossary
- **sci-ATAC-seq3**: Single-cell ATAC-seq using three rounds of combinatorial indexing
- **PCW**: Post-conceptional weeks; fetal developmental staging
- **TF motif**: Transcription factor DNA binding sequence motif; accessible regions enriched for motifs predict TF activity
