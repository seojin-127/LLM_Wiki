## One-line Summary
CHD8 haploinsufficiency in human cerebral organoids causes macrocephaly-like expansion, accelerates inhibitory neuron generation, delays excitatory neuron production, and disrupts mRNA processing in post-mitotic neurons — with temporally restricted effects on radial glia proliferation.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell Reports
- Published: 5 April 2022

## 2. Key Contributions
- CHD8 haploinsufficiency → macrocephaly-like organoid phenotype (enlarged size)
- Accelerated inhibitory neuron production + delayed excitatory neuron production (E/I imbalance)
- Temporally restricted cell-type-specific proliferation effect on radial glia
- mRNA processing (splicing) dysregulation in post-mitotic neurons
- Mechanistic detail: CHD8 regulates differentiation trajectory timing, not just gene expression

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- CRISPR CHD8 haploinsufficiency in cerebral organoids
- scRNA-seq time course + cell type composition analysis
- Organoid size measurement (macrocephaly phenotype)
- mRNA processing: splicing analysis from RNA-seq
- Comparison to controls at multiple developmental time points

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Macrocephaly-like: CHD8 mutant organoids significantly larger
- Inhibitory neuron acceleration: earlier production of GABAergic progenitors
- Excitatory neuron delay: reduced dorsal cortical plate neuron generation early
- Radial glia: cell-type-specific, temporally restricted proliferation changes
- mRNA processing: aberrant splicing in post-mitotic CHD8 mutant neurons

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Cerebral organoids: limited circuit complexity; no functional readout
- CRISPR haploinsufficiency: may not reflect all CHD8 mutation types
- mRNA processing mechanism downstream of CHD8 not fully characterized

## 6. Related Work
- Paulsen et al. 2022: CHD8 + 2 other ASD genes; convergence on same E/I timing phenotype
- Li et al. 2023 (CHOOSE): CHD8 among 36 screened genes
- Jin et al. 2020: CHD8 in vivo Perturb-Seq

## 7. Glossary
- **CHD8**: Chromodomain helicase DNA binding protein 8 — one of the highest-confidence ASD risk genes
- **Macrocephaly**: Enlarged head circumference; associated with CHD8 mutations in ASD
- **mRNA processing**: Splicing and post-transcriptional regulation; CHD8 regulates this in neurons
- **E/I imbalance**: Excitatory/inhibitory neuron ratio imbalance; hallmark of multiple ASD models
