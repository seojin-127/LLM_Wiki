## One-line Summary
Human cortical organoids transplanted into newborn rat somatosensory cortex develop mature cell types, integrate into sensory and motivation circuits, receive thalamocortical and corticocortical inputs, show sensory responses in vivo, and extend axons throughout rat brain; optogenetic activation drives reward-seeking behavior.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: October 2022

## 2. Key Contributions
- hCO transplanted into newborn athymic rat somatosensory cortex → mature cell types + circuit integration
- MRI: organoid growth tracked post-transplantation across stem cell lines
- snRNA-seq: progression of corticogenesis + activity-dependent transcriptional programs
- Transplanted neurons: more complex morphological, synaptic, intrinsic membrane properties than in vitro
- Timothy syndrome (CACNA1C) circuit defects uncovered (not detectable in vitro)
- Thalamocortical + corticocortical inputs → sensory responses in human cells (in vivo recordings)
- Axonal outgrowth throughout rat brain; optogenetic activation drives reward-seeking behavior

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- hCO from multiple iPSC lines transplanted into newborn (P1) athymic rat somatosensory cortex
- MRI for in vivo organoid growth monitoring; snRNA-seq for transcriptomic maturation
- Anatomical + functional tracing (rabies virus, thalamic injection)
- In vivo electrophysiology; patch clamp for intrinsic membrane properties

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- In vivo maturation: more complex neuron morphology, synaptic density, membrane properties
- Activity-dependent gene programs activated in transplanted neurons
- Thalamocortical + corticocortical inputs documented anatomically and functionally
- Sensory-evoked responses in transplanted human neurons (whisker stimulation)
- Optogenetic activation → real-time place preference (reward-seeking behavior)
- Timothy syndrome: aberrant activity patterns not seen in standard in vitro organoids

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Athymic immunocompromised rats; immune system influence not assessed
- Transplant into neonatal brain; adult transplantation may differ
- Long-term stability and safety unknown

## 6. Related Work
- Mansour et al. 2018: earlier in vivo transplantation model with vascularization
- Birey et al. 2017: assembloid for interneuron migration (alternative circuit model)
- Pasca et al. 2015: original hCS protocol

## 7. Glossary
- **Athymic rat**: Nude rat lacking T cells; allows xenograft without rejection
- **Thalamocortical input**: Sensory information from thalamus to cortex; primary sensory pathway
- **Activity-dependent transcription**: Gene expression changes driven by neuronal activity
- **Timothy syndrome**: CACNA1C gain-of-function; severe ASD + Long QT syndrome
