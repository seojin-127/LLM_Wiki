## One-line Summary
Single-cell and spatial transcriptomics of human gastrulation (CS7–CS13) and early brain development reveals NE-to-RG transformation, regional RG subtypes, and human-specific transcriptional features absent in mouse.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell Stem Cell
- Published: 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- First single-cell + spatial atlas of human gastrulation and neural tube formation (Carnegie stages 7–13)
- Traces neuroepithelium (NE) → radial glia (RG) transformation at single-cell resolution
- Identifies spatially distinct RG subtypes along anterior-posterior and dorsal-ventral axes
- Reveals human-specific transcriptional signatures not present in mouse gastrulation data
- Maps early patterning of forebrain, midbrain, hindbrain, and spinal cord progenitors

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq) of dissected Carnegie stage 7–13 human embryos
- Spatial transcriptomics (10x Visium) for in situ regional validation
- Cross-species comparison with published mouse gastrulation datasets
- Trajectory analysis from NE through early RG specification

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Continuous NE→RG transition captured with intermediate states
- Spatially resolved RG subtypes: forebrain vs. hindbrain vs. spinal progenitors show distinct TF programs
- Human-specific genes enriched in early neural progenitors (not conserved in mouse)
- Signaling pathways (WNT, FGF, SHH) show region-specific gradients matching known developmental biology

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Carnegie stage embryos are rare and small; limited cell numbers per stage
- Spatial resolution of Visium limits fine-scale anatomical mapping
- Cross-species comparison limited to available mouse datasets

## 6. Related Work
- Taverna et al. 2014: RG biology review (complementary)
- Braun et al. 2023: first-trimester comprehensive atlas (later stages)
- Mannens et al. 2025: chromatin accessibility in first-trimester brain

## 7. Glossary
- **Carnegie stage (CS)**: Standardized human embryonic staging system; CS7 ≈ gastrulation onset, CS13 ≈ ~5 weeks post-fertilization
- **NE (neuroepithelium)**: Early pseudostratified epithelial cells lining the neural tube; precursors to radial glia
- **RG (radial glia)**: Primary neural stem cells of the developing brain; arise from NE
