## One-line Summary
scRNA-seq comparison of primary human cortical cells vs. organoids shows organoids lack molecular subtypes and areal specification; ectopic cellular stress pathways impair subtype identity; transplantation into mouse cortex alleviates organoid stress and rescues subtype defects.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: February 2020

## 2. Key Contributions
- scRNA-seq of primary human cortical cells across developmental periods and cortical areas
- Organoids: broad cell classes present but molecular subtypes and areal specification absent
- Ectopic cellular stress pathways activated in organoids → impairs cell-type specification
- Transplantation into mouse cortex: reduces stress and rescues subtype defects
- Framework for evaluating organoid accuracy vs. primary tissue

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq of primary human fetal cortical cells + parallel cortical organoid cells
- Comparison across developmental periods (early fetal to late fetal) and cortical areas
- Transplantation experiment: cortical organoid cells transplanted into mouse cortex

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Primary cortex: diverse progenitor subtypes, mature neuron subtypes, areal specification
- Organoids: broad classes (progenitors, neurons, glia) but not molecular subtypes
- Areal molecular signatures emerge in organoids but are not spatially segregated
- Stress pathways (UPR, oxidative stress) ectopically activated in organoids
- Transplantation → reduced stress gene expression → partial subtype specification recovery

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Primary tissue comparison from limited gestational ages
- Transplantation model: immunocompromised mice; host influence on maturation
- Stress identification based on transcriptomics; mechanism not fully resolved

## 6. Related Work
- He et al. 2024 (HNOCA): quantifies cell stress as universal in vitro artifact
- Uzquiano et al. 2022: organoid cell class identity matches endogenous (complementary/contrasting)
- Mansour et al. 2018: in vivo vascularized organoid (alternative to transplantation)

## 7. Glossary
- **UPR**: Unfolded protein response — ER stress pathway; ectopically activated in organoids
- **Molecular subtype**: Fine-grained cell identity defined by specific marker gene combinations
- **Areal specification**: Cortical neuron identity specific to a particular cortical area (e.g., visual vs. motor cortex)
