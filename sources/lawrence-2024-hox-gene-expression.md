## One-line Summary
Single-cell and spatial transcriptomics atlas of the developing human fetal spine reveals that neural crest derivatives retain the HOX code of their anatomical origin while adopting the HOX code of their destination; identifies spinal cord dorsal/ventral axial patterning and HOX collinearity patterns.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Communications
- Published: 2024

## 2. Key Contributions
- First single-cell + spatial transcriptomics atlas of the developing human fetal spine
- Neural crest derivatives: unexpectedly retain anatomical HOX code of origin while adopting destination code (HOX "source code")
- Trend confirmed across multiple organs (not just spine)
- Spinal cord axial plane: distinct HOX patterns in ventral and dorsal domains; insights into motor pool organization
- Loss of collinearity in HOXB genes in specific spinal cord domains

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Single-cell RNA-seq of fetal spine (multiple developmental stages)
- Spatial transcriptomics across spinal cord sections
- In situ sequencing for validation
- HOX gene expression analysis across dissected anatomical levels

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Neural crest derivatives: dual HOX code (origin + destination); HOX "source code" concept
- Multi-organ validation of neural crest HOX retention
- Ventral spinal cord: motor pool HOX organization mapped
- HOXB genes: loss of collinearity in specific dorsal domains

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Fetal tissue; adult HOX dynamics not examined
- Neural crest HOX retention: mechanism not fully elucidated
- Spatial resolution limits for small cell populations in spinal cord

## 6. Related Work
- Cao et al. 2020: human fetal cell atlas (multi-organ context)
- Kiecker & Niehrs 2001: Wnt/β-catenin in AP patterning (related HOX upstream signaling)

## 7. Glossary
- **HOX genes**: Homeobox transcription factors; master regulators of anteroposterior body axis identity
- **Collinearity**: Property of HOX clusters where 3'→5' position correlates with rostral→caudal expression
- **Neural crest**: Migratory multipotent stem cells derived from neural plate border
- **Motor pool**: Group of motor neurons innervating a specific muscle; HOX-code dependent
