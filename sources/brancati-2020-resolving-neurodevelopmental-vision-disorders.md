## One-line Summary
Perspective article reviewing how single-cell multi-omics applied to brain and retina organoids can characterize features and limitations of organoid models and advance the study of congenital brain malformations and vision disorders.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Neuron
- Published: 2020

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reviews the state of single-cell multi-omics in brain and retinal organoid systems
- Identifies how single-cell tools reveal organoid fidelity and limitations vs. in vivo tissue
- Discusses applications to congenital brain malformations (e.g., microcephaly, lissencephaly) and vision disorders
- Proposes directions for improving organoid protocols using single-cell readouts
- Compares organoid to animal model limitations (lack of fovea in mice; near-lissencephalic marmoset)

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Perspective/review article; no primary data
- Synthesizes literature on scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq, and multiomics in brain/retina organoids

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Single-cell tools reveal organoid-specific artifacts (e.g., stress signatures, incomplete patterning)
- Multi-omics can match organoid cell types to in vivo counterparts
- Vision organoids (retinal organoids) reviewed alongside brain organoids

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Perspective only: no experimental validation
- Field rapidly evolving since 2020; many methods cited have since been superseded

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013: cerebral organoid foundational protocol
- Bhaduri et al. 2020: cell stress in cortical organoids
- Fleck et al. 2023: organoid GRN inference with Pando

## 7. Glossary
- **NHP**: Non-human primate — model organism with closer brain anatomy to humans
- **Lissencephalic**: Smooth-brained (lacking cortical folds/gyri) — characteristic of mice; limits cortical folding studies
- **Fovea**: Central region of retina for high-acuity vision; absent in mice, present in humans and marmosets
