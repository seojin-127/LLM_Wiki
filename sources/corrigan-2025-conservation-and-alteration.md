## One-line Summary
Cross-species scRNA-seq of inhibitory neurons across 10 mammalian species (160 Myr of divergence) shows that TAC3 striatal interneurons — previously thought primate-specific — are conserved across placental mammals, with changes in anatomical redistribution and marker gene expression rather than novel cell-type origins.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: 6 November 2025 (Vol. 647)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Surveyed inhibitory neuron diversity in developing cortex and striatum of 10 mammalian species (primates, rodents, ungulates, marsupials) + adult data
- TAC3 initial class is MGE-derived and ancestral (not primate-specific); present in pig, ferret cortex and conserved in mice as Tac2/Th interneurons
- Mouse striatum has conservation of TAC3/Tac2 initial class, obscured by reduced Tac2 expression and gained Th expression
- Evolution of neuronal cell types acts through redistribution and fate refinement, not novel precursor derivation

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq (10x Genomics) of developing cortex/striatum across Eutheria and Marsupialia
- Downsampling + Harmony co-embedding for cross-species integration
- Leiden clustering; classification by conserved MGE/LGE/CGE transcription factors
- RNAscope in situ hybridization for spatial validation in pig, ferret, porpoise
- Pseudotime trajectory analysis across species

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Initial classes of inhibitory neurons highly correlated across species; segregate by cell type, not species
- TAC3 initial class conserved in Laurasiatheria (pig, ferret) by RNAscope
- Mouse targeted MGE precursor enrichment reveals Tac2 population in ventromedial striatum
- Primate TAC3 and mouse Th striatal interneurons are homologous based on shared gene expression programs

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Functional consequences of TAC3/Th striatal interneuron differences across species unknown
- Limited by available embryonic tissue for some species
- Marker gene turnover may reflect neutral drift or functional specialization (unresolved)

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **TAC3**: Tachykinin 3 — neuropeptide gene marking a primate striatal interneuron population
- **MGE**: Medial ganglionic eminence — major source of cortical/striatal inhibitory neurons
- **Initial class**: Newborn neuron cell state before extrinsic fate refinement to terminal class
- **Harmony**: Computational method for cross-dataset/cross-species single-cell integration
