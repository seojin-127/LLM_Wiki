## One-line Summary
PsychENCODE cortical organoid transcriptome + epigenome atlas identifies 96,375 gene-linked enhancers during early cortical development; activating (A-reg) and repressive (R-reg) enhancer modules enriched for ASD de novo variants; organoids validate as a model for early embryonic cortex.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Science
- Published: December 2018 (PsychENCODE)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Transcriptome + H3K27ac/H3K27me3/H3K4me3 ChIP-seq time course in cortical organoids (5–16 PCW equivalent) + isogenic fetal cortex
- 96,375 gene-linked enhancers; 49,640 organoid-specific (not active in mid-fetal brain)
- WGCNA: 54 co-expressed gene modules and 29 co-active enhancer modules — 6 and 4 supermodules respectively
- A-reg (activating) vs. R-reg (repressive) enhancer categories stratified by gene expression correlation
- ASD de novo variants enriched in enhancers targeting homeodomain, Hes1, NR4A2, Sox3, NFIX binding sites
- Organoids model embryonic cortex (5–16 PCW) reliably; greatest change at stem cell → progenitor transition

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- hiPSC-derived cortical organoids + isogenic fetal brain tissue
- RNA-seq time course + ChIP-seq (H3K27ac, H3K27me3, H3K4me3)
- Hi-C for promoter-enhancer linking; WGCNA for co-expression and co-activity networks
- ASD de novo variant enrichment analysis in enhancer modules

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 327,877 putative enhancers identified; 96,375 linked to target genes via Hi-C
- 49,640 enhancers active in organoids but not mid-fetal brain → early specification role
- Human-lineage-gained enhancers active earliest (stem cell stage) targeting radial glia growth genes
- Convergence: several enhancer modules co-activate with gene expression modules
- ASD-linked de novo variants disrupt specific TF binding sites (homeodomain, Hes1, NR4A2, Sox3, NFIX)

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Organoids model only 5–16 PCW window; later stages not captured
- ChIP-seq on bulk organoid tissue — limited cell-type resolution
- Functional validation of specific A-reg/R-reg enhancers needed

## 7. Glossary
- **A-reg**: Activating enhancer regulators — enhancers whose activity positively correlates with linked gene expression
- **R-reg**: Repressive enhancer regulators — enhancers with negative correlation to linked gene expression
- **WGCNA**: Weighted gene co-expression network analysis
- **PCW**: Post-conception weeks
- **Supermodule**: Meta-cluster of co-expressed/co-active modules sharing temporal pattern
