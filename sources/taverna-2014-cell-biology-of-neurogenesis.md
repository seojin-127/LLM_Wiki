## One-line Summary
Comprehensive review of the cell biology of cortical neural stem and progenitor cells, covering their classification, polarity, subcellular features, modes of division, fate determination, and evolutionary differences underlying neocortex expansion.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Annual Review of Cell and Developmental Biology
- Published: 2014

## 2. Key Contributions
- Systematic classification criteria for cortical stem and progenitor cell types (NE, RG, oRG/OSVZ progenitors, IPs)
- Detailed analysis of cell polarity and subcellular features relevant to division modes and daughter cell fate
- Tissue-level context: extracellular signals, cell-cell interactions governing progenitor behavior
- Cross-species comparison of cortical progenitor diversity: key evolutionary differences underlying gyrification and neocortex expansion

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Review article; synthesis of molecular, cell biological, and developmental genetics literature
- Covers: interkinetic nuclear migration (INM), apical vs. basal process anchoring, cilia structure, cell cycle regulation, asymmetric vs. symmetric divisions

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Neuroepithelial (NE) cells → radial glia (RG) → basal progenitors (IPs, oRG) lineage established
- oRG (outer radial glia) cells enriched in gyrencephalic species (human, ferret) vs. lissencephalic (mouse); key for cortical expansion
- Cilia, adherens junctions, and basal processes are critical determinants of progenitor maintenance vs. differentiation
- INM is mechanistically linked to cell cycle phase and division mode

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Review of 2014 literature; field has advanced considerably (scRNA-seq era)
- Some mechanistic models remain to be fully validated

## 6. Related Work
- Huttner lab: foundational work on NE/RG biology
- Götz lab: radial glia as neural stem cells
- Fietz & Huttner 2011: OSVZ and oRG discovery

## 7. Glossary
- **NE**: Neuroepithelial cell — earliest cortical progenitor
- **RG**: Radial glia — major cortical progenitor; apically anchored
- **oRG**: Outer radial glia (bRG, OSVZ progenitor) — key for gyrification
- **IP**: Intermediate progenitor — transient amplifying progenitor in SVZ
- **INM**: Interkinetic nuclear migration — apico-basal nuclear movement tied to cell cycle
- **OSVZ**: Outer subventricular zone — expanded progenitor zone in gyrencephalic brains
