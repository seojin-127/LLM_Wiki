## One-line Summary
Long-term live light-sheet microscopy of fluorescently labelled unguided human brain organoids enables quantitative tracking of tissue morphology, cell behaviors, and subcellular features during neuroepithelial induction, lumenization, and brain regionalization; extrinsic matrix and mechanosensing (WNT/YAP1) control lumen expansion and telencephalon formation.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: 28 August 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- First long-term live light-sheet microscopy platform for brain organoids (multi-mosaic, dual-channel labeling)
- Quantitative tracking of actin, tubulin, plasma membrane, nucleus dynamics over weeks
- Morphodynamic atlas: neuroepithelial induction → maturation → lumenization → regionalization
- Extrinsic matrix (Matrigel) enhances lumen expansion and telencephalon formation; absence → neural crest/caudalized fate
- WNT/YAP1 (Hippo) signaling links matrix-mechanosensing to regional brain specification
- WLS marks earliest emergence of non-telencephalic brain regions

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Fluorescently labelled hiPSCs (multi-mosaic + multi-protein strategy)
- Long-term light-sheet microscopy: weeks of continuous imaging
- Computational demultiplexing: separate fluorescent channels from overlapping signals
- Morphometric quantification: cell shape, alignment, lumen dynamics
- scRNA-seq integration: transcript programs linked to morphological transitions
- Comparison: +/- extrinsic matrix (Matrigel)

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Lumenal expansion: associated with extracellular matrix pathway + mechanosensing gene programs
- Matrix-free organoids: increased neural crest + caudalized (non-telencephalic) identity
- WLS expression: earliest spatial marker of non-telencephalic brain regions
- WNT pathway: mediates matrix-induced regional guidance
- YAP1 (Hippo): mechanosensing link between matrix and lumen morphogenesis

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Unguided organoids: inherent variability in regionalization
- Light-sheet imaging requires specialized equipment
- Mechanistic dissection of matrix → WNT/YAP1 → regionalization pathway ongoing

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013: original cerebral organoid (matrix-based protocol)
- Giandomenico et al. 2019: ALI-COs (improved survival)
- He et al. 2024 (HNOCA): atlas for categorizing organoid states (Treutlein lab)

## 7. Glossary
- **Light-sheet microscopy**: Illuminates a thin plane; low phototoxicity for long-term live imaging
- **Lumenization**: Formation and expansion of fluid-filled lumens within neuroepithelium
- **WLS**: Wntless — Wnt ligand secretion mediator; marks non-telencephalic emergence
- **YAP1**: Hippo pathway effector — mechanosensing transcriptional co-activator
- **Morphodynamics**: Quantitative study of tissue shape changes over time
