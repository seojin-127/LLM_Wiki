## One-line Summary
Single-nucleus RNA-seq and ATAC-seq atlas of human prefrontal cortex from gestation to adulthood reveals protracted, cell-type-specific maturation dynamics and identifies gene regulatory networks predicting disease origins; also benchmarks long-term brain organoids against postnatal maturity.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell
- Published: November 10, 2022 (Vol. 185, 4428–4447)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Generated snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq atlas of human PFC from gestation through adulthood (spanning prenatal to adult)
- Found protracted and diverse cell-type-specific timing of development to a mature state
- RNA dynamics and regulators predict cellular and temporal origins of brain diseases
- Integration with organoid data reveals few postnatal-mature neurons in long-term brain organoids
- Identified major gene expression reconfiguration at prenatal-to-postnatal transition, followed by continuous reconfiguration into adulthood

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- snRNA-seq and snATAC-seq of human PFC across developmental time points (gestation → adulthood)
- Cell-type resolution trajectory analysis; timing of maturation per cell type
- Regulatory network inference (TF regulons, chromatin accessibility)
- Integration with long-term brain organoid data to assess in vitro maturation fidelity

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Prenatal-to-postnatal transition marks major gene expression reconfiguration
- Continuous transcriptomic reconfiguration continues into adulthood across all cell types
- Cell types differ in timing and pace of maturation
- Long-term organoids (even >1 year) contain few neurons matching postnatal maturity
- Disease-relevant gene regulatory networks identified from developmental trajectories

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Human tissue samples vary in quality; limited intermediate postnatal time points
- snRNA-seq misses cytoplasmic transcripts; chromatin accessibility is a proxy for regulatory activity

## 6. Related Work
- Gordon et al. 2021 (Nature Neuroscience): long-term hCS maturation
- Kanton et al. 2019: cross-species organoid atlas
- Brain development single-cell atlases (Nowakowski et al., Braun et al.)

## 7. Glossary
- **snATAC-seq**: Single-nucleus ATAC-seq — chromatin accessibility at single-nucleus resolution
- **PFC**: Prefrontal cortex
- **Regulon**: Set of genes regulated by a common transcription factor
- **Prenatal-to-postnatal transition**: Major biological switch at birth affecting brain gene expression globally
