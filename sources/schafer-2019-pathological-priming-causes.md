## One-line Summary
iPSC-derived neurons from ASD individuals show pathological priming — premature activation of late developmental gene networks in neural stem cells — causing heterochronicity in gene expression relative to controls, revealing a convergent mechanism across idiopathic ASD.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Neuroscience
- Published: 2019

## 2. Key Contributions
- Described "pathological priming": premature expression of late developmental transcriptional programs in ASD iPSC-derived NSCs
- Identified heterochronicity in developmental gene network timing in ASD neurons vs controls
- Used time-series transcriptomic analysis of iPSC → NSC → neuron differentiation from ASD individuals
- Convergent priming phenotype across idiopathic ASD (not single-gene)
- Implicated specific fetal cortical development windows as ASD-vulnerable periods

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- iPSC generation from idiopathic ASD individuals and neurotypical controls
- Neural stem cell (NSC) and neuron differentiation time series
- Bulk RNA-seq at multiple differentiation stages
- Gene network analysis: weighted gene co-expression networks (WGCNA)
- Comparison with human fetal brain gene expression datasets

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- ASD NSCs show premature activation of late neurodevelopmental transcriptional programs
- Heterochronicity: timing of gene network activation is off relative to controls
- Convergent pathological priming across multiple idiopathic ASD individuals
- Temporal mapping identifies specific fetal cortical development stages as ASD-vulnerable

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Bulk RNA-seq misses cell-type heterogeneity
- iPSC-derived neurons may not fully recapitulate in vivo ASD pathology
- Idiopathic ASD is heterogeneous; causality of priming uncertain

## 6. Related Work
- Jin et al. 2020 (Science): in vivo Perturb-Seq of ASD risk genes

## 7. Glossary
- **Pathological priming**: Premature activation of late developmental gene networks in early progenitors
- **Heterochronicity**: Altered timing of developmental events relative to normal sequence
- **NSC**: Neural stem cell
- **Idiopathic ASD**: ASD without identified single-gene cause (polygenic/unknown etiology)
