## One-line Summary
Spl-ISO-Seq: spatial long-read sequencing at near-single-cell resolution of human post-mortem visual cortex reveals layer 4 excitatory neurons have the most developmentally regulated splicing changes during puberty, with alternative splicing linked to post-synaptic function.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Communications
- Published: 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Spl-ISO-Seq: spatial long-read method (selects long cDNAs, 2-3× longer reads than standard)
- Near-single-cell resolution spatial isoform mapping in complex tissue
- Spl-ISOquant: custom software for spatial long-read analysis
- Pre- vs. post-puberty human visual cortex (8–11 vs. 16–19 years)
- Layer 4 excitatory neurons: most developmentally regulated splicing changes and polyA sites
- Cortex has stronger splicing regulation than white matter; oligodendrocytes stronger in WM
- Developmental splicing changes linked to post-synaptic structure and function

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Spl-ISO-Seq: selective enrichment of long cDNAs + PacBio long-read sequencing
- Spl-ISOquant: spatial long-read quantification software
- Human post-mortem visual cortex: pre-puberty (8–11 years) vs. post-puberty (16–19 years)
- Near-single-cell spatial resolution (cell-level isoform assignment)

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Spl-ISO-Seq: 2-3× longer reads than standard spatial long-read preparations
- Cortex vs. white matter: stronger splicing and polyA regulation in cortex
- Oligodendrocyte regulation: stronger in white matter
- Layer 4 excitatory neurons: most developmental splicing changes across cortical layers
- Developmentally regulated exons enriched downstream of repeat elements
- Alternative splicing changes linked to post-synaptic structure and function

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Post-mortem human tissue; RNA quality may vary
- Near-single-cell (not true single-cell) spatial resolution
- Two age groups only; continuous developmental trajectory not captured

## 6. Related Work
- Joglekar et al. 2024: single-cell long-read brain isoform atlas (complementary)
- Foord referenced in Joglekar 2024 as spatial complement

## 7. Glossary
- **Spl-ISO-Seq**: Spatial Isoform Sequencing — spatial + long-read RNA-seq method
- **Spl-ISOquant**: Software for spatial long-read quantification
- **PolyA site**: Polyadenylation site; alternative polyA affects mRNA stability and isoform diversity
- **Layer 4**: Granular layer of neocortex; primary recipient of thalamocortical sensory input
