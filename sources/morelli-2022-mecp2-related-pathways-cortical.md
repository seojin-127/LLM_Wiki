## One-line Summary
Cortical organoids from DM1 patient iPSCs show CUG foci causing CELF2 hyperphosphorylation, leading to misregulation of MECP2 pathway genes in glutamatergic neurons; convergent defects with MECP2-null organoids suggest shared ASD-related mechanisms in DM1.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Science Translational Medicine
- Published: 29 June 2022 (Vol. 14, eabn2375)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Modeled DM1 neurodevelopmental pathology in 3D cortical organoids from patient iPSCs
- CUG repeat foci → CELF2 hyperphosphorylation → reduced CELF2-RNA substrate interactions
- scRNA-seq + eCLIP reveals MECP2 pathway gene misregulation in glutamatergic neurons
- Convergent molecular/cellular defects (glutamate toxicity, neuronal loss) between DM1 and MECP2(y/-) organoids
- Identified NMDA antagonists as potential treatment for DM1 neuronal defects

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- 3D cortical organoid differentiation from DM1 patient iPSCs
- scRNA-seq for cell-type-specific transcriptomic profiling
- eCLIP (enhanced CLIP) for CELF2 RNA binding targets
- Comparison with MECP2(y/-) cortical organoids
- Functional assays: glutamate toxicity, neuronal viability

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- DM1 organoids: CUG foci present early in maturation → CELF2 hyperphosphorylation
- CELF2 reduced binding → MECP2 pathway gene misregulation in glutamatergic neurons
- Convergent phenotypes with Rett syndrome (MECP2) organoids
- NMDA antagonists rescue neuronal defects in DM1 organoids

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Organoid model; lacks microglia/vasculature
- DM1 is heterogeneous; single iPSC line limits generalizability
- In vivo validation of NMDA antagonist treatment needed

## 6. Related Work
- MECP2 and Rett syndrome (RTT)
- DM1 molecular biology (MBNL, CUGBP)
- ASD convergence studies (Satterstrom, Jin et al. 2020)

## 7. Glossary
- **DM1**: Myotonic dystrophy type 1 — caused by CTG trinucleotide repeat expansion in DMPK 3'UTR
- **CUG foci**: Nuclear RNA foci formed by expanded CUG repeats sequestering RNA-binding proteins
- **CELF2**: RNA-binding protein dysregulated in DM1
- **MECP2**: Methyl-CpG binding protein 2 — loss-of-function causes Rett syndrome
- **eCLIP**: Enhanced CLIP-seq — maps protein-RNA interactions transcriptome-wide
