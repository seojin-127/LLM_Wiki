## One-line Summary
Comprehensive atlas of adult mouse brain cell types built by pairing high-throughput snRNA-seq from 92 anatomical dissectates (55 mice) with Slide-seq spatial transcriptomics, revealing cell type composition of each neuroanatomical structure, neuropeptide/neurotransmitter maps, and heritability enrichment for brain diseases.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: 14 December 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- First comprehensive atlas mapping cell type identity to specific neuroanatomical structures across the whole adult mouse brain
- Paired snRNA-seq from 92 dissected anatomical regions (55 mice) with Slide-seq spatial transcriptomics
- Cell type diversity highest in midbrain, hindbrain, and hypothalamus; most clusters require ≥3 discrete markers
- Comprehensive neuropeptide and neurotransmitter signaling characterization
- Heritability enrichment of neurological and psychiatric phenotypes mapped to specific cell types
- Online resource: www.BrainCellData.org; framework for genetic access to each cell type

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- High-throughput snRNA-seq pipeline (high capture + recovery efficiency) from 92 anatomical dissectates
- Slide-seq (near-cellular resolution spatial transcriptomics) across whole brain
- Integration of snRNA-seq clusters with Slide-seq spatial positions
- Neuropeptide/neurotransmitter marker analysis; activity-regulated gene expression
- Heritability enrichment: S-LDSC partitioned heritability analysis

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Midbrain, hindbrain, and hypothalamus: highest cell type diversity; most clusters require ≥3 markers
- Cortex: lower diversity, simpler marker sets
- Comprehensive neuropeptide/neurotransmitter co-expression atlas
- Region-specific activity-regulated gene expression programs identified
- GWAS heritability enriched in specific cell types for schizophrenia, depression, neurological traits

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Adult brain only; developmental dynamics not captured
- Dissected regions may mix cell types at boundaries
- Slide-seq resolution (~10 µm) near-cellular but not single-cell

## 6. Related Work
- Zhang et al. 2023 (MERFISH whole brain atlas): complementary spatial approach
- Yao et al. 2023: BICCN adult mouse brain atlas
- Chen et al. 2024: brain atlas integrating single-cell + spatial

## 7. Glossary
- **Slide-seq**: Spatial transcriptomics method with near-cellular resolution barcoded beads
- **snRNA-seq**: Single-nucleus RNA sequencing; works on frozen tissue
- **S-LDSC**: Stratified linkage disequilibrium score regression for heritability partitioning
- **Cytoarchitecture**: Spatial organization of cell types within a tissue structure
