## One-line Summary
Prospective lineage tracing of 6,402 human primary cortical progenitors during neurogenesis and gliogenesis; shows progenitors switch from glutamatergic to GABAergic output at midgestation; truncated RG maintain glutamatergic potential for extended period; late-born truncated RG neurons show deep-layer molecular features.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: November 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Prospective lineage tracing of 6,402 progenitor cells in primary human cortical tissue
- First lineage-resolved map of human cortical development during neurogenesis and gliogenesis
- Cortical progenitors switch from glutamatergic to GABAergic neurogenesis at midgestation
- Midgestation coincides with onset of oligodendrocyte generation
- Truncated radial glia (tRG): maintain glutamatergic neurogenic potential for protracted period
- Late-born tRG-derived glutamatergic neurons: molecular features of deep cortical layer neurons + subplate expansion

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Prospective lineage tracing: lentiviral clonal barcoding of cortical progenitors in human primary tissue
- 6,402 progenitor cells traced; single-cell RNA-seq readout per clone
- Neurogenesis + gliogenesis window covered

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Progenitor switch: glutamatergic → GABAergic neurogenesis at midgestation
- OPC generation onset coincides with glutamatergic→GABAergic switch
- tRG: maintain glutamatergic potential beyond the switch
- Late-born tRG neurons: deep-layer molecular features; may contribute to subplate expansion during midgestation

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Primary human tissue lineage tracing: limited to ex vivo window
- Clonal output may not capture rare progenitor types
- tRG functional contribution to subplate requires in vivo validation

## 6. Related Work
- Keefe in Nowakowski lab; related to Bhaduri et al. and Wang et al. 2025
- Braun et al. 2023: first-trimester atlas (earlier stages)

## 7. Glossary
- **Truncated radial glia (tRG)**: Radial glia that lose their basal process; found mainly in outer SVZ; neurogenic and gliogenic
- **Prospective lineage tracing**: Tracking cell progeny forward in time from a labeled progenitor
- **Subplate**: Transient cortical layer in development; important for thalamocortical axon targeting
