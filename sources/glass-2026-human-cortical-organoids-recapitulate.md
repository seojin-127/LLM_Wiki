## One-line Summary
iPSC-derived human cortical organoids from longitudinally imaged infants show that early in vitro fate decisions (cell-type proportions, cell cycle genes, organoid size) correlate with individual differences in infant cortical surface area in vivo.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell Stem Cell
- Published: January 2026

## 2. Key Contributions
- Cortical organoids generated from participants with longitudinal infant brain MRI data
- Organoid cell-type proportions and cross-sectional area growth correlate with infant cortical surface area in vivo
- Cell cycle genes in early neural progenitors associate with inter-individual variability in cortical growth
- Hem and choroid plexus proportions correlate with cortical surface area
- Validates organoids as a model system that reflects individual genetic background effects on brain development

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- iPSC-derived cortical organoids from genetically diverse participants
- Paired with longitudinal infant brain imaging (MRI) data from the Infant Brain Imaging Study (IBIS) Network
- scRNA-seq of organoids; quantification of cell-type proportions and organoid size metrics

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Early fate decisions in vitro (cell-type proportions) correlate with cortical surface area in vivo
- Organoid cross-sectional area growth associated with individual cortical surface area measurements
- Hem and choroid plexus proportions linked to inter-individual brain growth variability
- Cell cycle gene expression in early neural progenitors reflects individual differences

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Organoid model: limited resolution of cortical laminar identity and areal specificity
- Correlation study: mechanistic links between organoid and in vivo phenotypes require follow-up
- Brain MRI data from infancy only; later developmental time points not matched

## 6. Related Work
- Bhaduri et al. 2020: cell stress in organoids (complementary organoid quality control)
- Lancaster et al. 2013: cerebral organoid foundational protocol
- Revah et al. 2022: organoid maturation and circuit integration

## 7. Glossary
- **Cortical surface area**: MRI-measured brain phenotype with high heritability; differs across individuals
- **Hem**: Cortical hem — signaling center at the dorsomedial edge of the telencephalon; produces Wnt signals
- **Choroid plexus**: Epithelial structure producing cerebrospinal fluid; modeled in organoids
