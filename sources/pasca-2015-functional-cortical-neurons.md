## One-line Summary
Human cortical spheroids (hCS) — a simple, reproducible 3D cortical organoid from pluripotent stem cells — contain neurons from deep and superficial cortical layers mapping transcriptionally to fetal development, with electrophysiologically mature, synaptically connected neurons and nonreactive astrocytes.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Methods
- Published: July 2015

## 2. Key Contributions
- Original hCS (human cortical spheroid) protocol: reproducible, matrix-free 3D cortical organoid from hPSCs
- Laminated structure with both deep and superficial layer neurons (CTIP2+, SATB2+)
- Transcriptional mapping to in vivo fetal cortical development
- Electrophysiologically mature neurons: spontaneous activity, functional synapses, network activity in acute slices
- Nonreactive astrocytes — preserves astrocyte state better than 2D
- No Matrigel required: simpler than Lancaster protocol

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Directed differentiation of hPSCs/hiPSCs without Matrigel embedding
- Spheroid format: self-organizing in ultra-low attachment conditions
- Bulk RNA-seq comparison to in vivo fetal cortex
- Patch-clamp electrophysiology; calcium imaging; synaptic marker IHC

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Deep (CTIP2+) and superficial (SATB2+) layer neurons present
- Transcriptional correlation to 19–24 gestational week human fetal cortex
- Mature action potentials; spontaneous EPSCs/IPSCs; network bursting in acute slices
- Astrocytes: GFAP+, non-reactive morphology; support neuronal survival

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- No vascularization; limited to early developmental stages
- No inhibitory interneurons (forebrain-directed, not ventral)
- Batch variability across hPSC lines

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013: concurrent cerebral organoid method (unguided, with Matrigel)
- Gordon et al. 2021: long-term maturation of hCS
- Paulsen et al. 2022: ASD disease modeling in hCS

## 7. Glossary
- **hCS**: Human cortical spheroid — the specific organoid type introduced in this paper
- **CTIP2 (BCL11B)**: Deep cortical layer (L5/6) marker
- **SATB2**: Superficial cortical layer (L2/3/4) callosal neuron marker
- **Matrix-free**: No Matrigel/basement membrane extract; simpler and more reproducible
