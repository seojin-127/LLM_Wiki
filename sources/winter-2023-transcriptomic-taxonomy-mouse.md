## One-line Summary
Transcriptomic profiling of 65,002 mouse spinal projecting neurons (SPNs) from 32 brain regions identifies 76 region-specific types organized in three functional components; a LIM homeobox TF code parcellates reticulospinal neurons into 5 molecular populations.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: December 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- First brain-wide transcriptomic taxonomy of SPNs: 65,002 neurons, 76 region-specific types
- Three-component organization: (1) homogeneous excitatory SPNs (cortex/red nucleus/cerebellum) for point-to-point communication; (2) heterogeneous reticular formation SPNs for global spinal modulation; (3) modulatory neurons (neuropeptides/slow neurotransmitters) for gain-setting
- LIM homeobox TF code parcellates reticulospinal neurons into 5 distinct populations
- Transcriptional signatures of large-soma SPNs correlate with fast-firing electrophysiology

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq of retrogradely labeled SPNs (retrograde viral tracing + FACS)
- 32 source brain regions projecting to 24 spinal cord targets (225 source-to-target combinations)
- Integration with companion whole-brain atlas for projection-type mapping

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 76 region-specific SPN types mapped brain-wide
- Cortical/cerebellar SPNs: molecularly homogeneous excitatory; somatotopic spinal terminations
- Reticular formation SPNs: heterogeneous; broad spinal termination → global command relay
- Hypothalamic/midbrain/hindbrain modulatory SPNs: slow-acting neuropeptides for gain-setting
- LIM homeobox code: 5 molecularly/spatially distinct reticulospinal populations
- Large soma + fast-firing electrophysiology correlate with specific transcriptional signatures

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Mouse only; human SPN taxonomy lacking
- Retrograde labeling efficiency may miss low-abundance projection neurons
- Cross-species conservation of SPN types not established

## 6. Related Work
- Yao et al. 2023: Allen Brain Cell Atlas (companion reference for mapping)
- Van Velthoven et al. 2025: telencephalic GABAergic taxonomy (complementary)
- Langlieb et al. 2023: whole adult mouse brain cytoarchitecture

## 7. Glossary
- **SPN**: Spinal projecting neuron — neuron sending descending axon from brain to spinal cord
- **Reticulospinal**: Neurons from reticular formation projecting to spinal cord; important for locomotion
- **LIM homeobox TF**: Transcription factors with dual LIM + homeodomain; key for spinal cord motor neuron identity
