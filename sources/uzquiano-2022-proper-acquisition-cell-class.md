## One-line Summary
Single-cell transcriptomic, epigenetic, and spatial atlas of human cortical organoid development shows cell class identity acquisition matches endogenous cortex irrespective of metabolic state; identifies genes with predicted human-specific roles; callosal neuron diversity emerges early.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell
- Published: September 2022

## 2. Key Contributions
- Single-cell atlas: transcriptomics + epigenetics + spatial of human cortical organoids across development
- Cellular diversification in organoids closely correlates with endogenous cortical development
- Metabolic states (stress) do not substantially affect cell identity acquisition for most cell classes
- Pseudo-time trajectories in organoids predict human-specific regulatory mechanisms
- Callosal neuron transcriptional diversity emerges at early developmental stages

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq + single-cell ATAC-seq + spatial transcriptomics of hCS at multiple developmental stages
- Comparison to primary human cortical tissue for fidelity assessment
- Metabolic profiling to assess whether cell stress artifacts alter identity

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Organoid cell class identity acquisition closely matches endogenous cortex
- Cell metabolic state does not distort cell identity for major classes
- Pseudo-time analysis predicts genes with human-specific roles in fate specification
- Callosal neuron transcriptional diversity: detectable at early organoid stages
- Spatial validation confirms identity of organoid cell types

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Cell subtype specification (beyond class) may still be impaired in organoids
- Spatial coverage limited by current spatial methods
- Human-specific predictions require in vivo validation

## 6. Related Work
- Bhaduri et al. 2020: cell stress impairs molecular subtype specification in organoids (complementary/contrasting)
- He et al. 2024 (HNOCA): organoid fidelity benchmark

## 7. Glossary
- **Cell class**: Broad cell identity (e.g., excitatory neuron, interneuron, astrocyte) vs. cell subtype
- **Callosal neuron**: Excitatory neuron projecting via corpus callosum; supragranular layers
- **Metabolic state**: Cellular metabolic activity; correlates with stress artifacts in organoids
