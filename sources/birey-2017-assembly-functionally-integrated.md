## One-line Summary
First forebrain assembloid model: fusion of hCS (dorsal, glutamatergic) and hSS (ventral, GABAergic) spheroids enables modeling of human interneuron saltatory migration and functional circuit integration in vitro.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: May 2017

## 2. Key Contributions
- First assembloid system fusing pallial (hCS) and subpallial (hSS) spheroids
- Models saltatory migration of GABAergic interneurons from ventral to dorsal forebrain in vitro
- Functional circuit integration: GABAergic neurons integrate into hCS microcircuits
- Single-cell profiling: scRNA-seq of ~11,838 cells distinguishes hCS vs. hSS cell types
- Timothy syndrome (CACNA1C) interneurons show migration defects rescued by roscovitine

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- hSS: NKX2-1+ subpallial spheroids differentiated via WNT/SHH modulation + FGF2/EGF
- hCS: previously described pallial spheroids (Pasca et al. 2015)
- Assembloid: physical fusion of hCS + hSS; interneuron migration tracked by live imaging
- scRNA-seq (BD Resolve), electrophysiology (patch clamp, MEA)

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- hSS: GABAergic neurons (GABA, GAD67+), SST, calretinin, calbindin, late parvalbumin
- Assembloid: saltatory interneuron migration captured by live imaging
- Interneurons integrate functionally: inhibitory postsynaptic currents recorded in hCS neurons
- Timothy syndrome hSS: reduced saltatory migration; roscovitine partially rescues

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Assembloid fusion is physical juxtaposition; may not precisely replicate in vivo migration microenvironment
- Long maturation period (>200 days) for full interneuron subtype maturation
- Assembloid system lacks vascularization and thalamic inputs

## 6. Related Work
- Pasca et al. 2015: original hCS protocol (pallial spheroid)
- Revah et al. 2022: in vivo transplantation for circuit integration (complementary)
- Paulsen et al. 2022: ASD gene convergence on GABAergic/excitatory balance

## 7. Glossary
- **Assembloid**: Fusion of two region-specific organoids/spheroids to model inter-regional interactions
- **hCS**: Human cortical spheroid — pallial, glutamatergic
- **hSS**: Human subpallial spheroid — GABAergic interneuron-generating
- **Saltatory migration**: Jump-like interneuron migration mode characteristic of human interneurons
- **Timothy syndrome**: CACNA1C gain-of-function; severe ASD + cardiac arrhythmia
