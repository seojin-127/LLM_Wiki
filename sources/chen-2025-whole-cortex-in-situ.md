## One-line Summary
BARseq of 10.3M mouse cortical cells (4.2M neurons, 104 genes) reveals that transcriptomic type composition predicts cortical area identity; neonatal binocular enucleation shifts visual area transcriptomic profiles toward neighboring areas, showing sensory input sharpens area identity.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: November 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- BARseq applied whole-cortex: 10.3M cells, 4,194,658 neurons, 104 marker genes, 9 hemispheres
- Transcriptomic type composition highly predictive of cortical area identity
- Cortical modules: areas with similar transcriptomic compositions are highly interconnected
- Binocular enucleation: visual area transcriptomic profiles shift toward neighboring areas → sensory input sharpens area identity
- Proof-of-principle for large-scale in situ sequencing for brain-wide molecular architecture

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- BARseq: high-throughput, low-cost, reproducible in situ sequencing
- 104 cell-type marker genes in 10.3M cells across 9 mouse forebrain hemispheres
- De novo clustering of gene expression per neuron
- Neonatal binocular enucleation experiment for developmental perturbation

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Transcriptomic type composition predicts cortical area identity
- Cortical modules = areas with similar transcriptomic composition + high connectivity
- Binocular enucleation → visual area types shift to non-visual composition → input sharpens identity
- Consistent with prior scRNA-seq studies (Tasic et al., Allen Brain Atlas)

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- 104 genes (targeted panel); not whole transcriptome
- Mouse only; human cortical area identity mechanisms may differ
- Enucleation is coarse perturbation; input specificity mechanisms not resolved

## 6. Related Work
- Yao et al. 2023: Allen Brain Cell Atlas (scRNA reference for BARseq types)
- Tasic et al. 2018: cortical cell type atlas (prior context)

## 7. Glossary
- **BARseq**: Barcoded Anatomy Resolved by Sequencing — in situ sequencing with cell-type resolution across large tissue volumes
- **Cortical module**: Set of adjacent cortical areas sharing transcriptomic type composition and high connectivity
- **Binocular enucleation**: Surgical removal of both eyes at birth to eliminate visual input
