## One-line Summary
Human brain organoids transplanted into adult mouse brain undergo vascularization, progressive neuronal maturation, axonal outgrowth to host regions, and functional synaptic connectivity demonstrated by two-photon imaging and optogenetics.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Biotechnology
- Published: April 2018

## 2. Key Contributions
- First efficient in vivo engraftment model of hPSC-derived brain organoids into adult mouse brain
- Grafted organoids develop functional vasculature from host (authentic blood microenvironment, not just tubular networks)
- Progressive differentiation: NPCs → neurons → glia; axons project to multiple host brain regions
- Functional demonstration: two-photon imaging shows active neuronal networks and blood flow; optogenetics reveals graft-to-host synaptic connectivity

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- GFP+ hESC-derived cerebral organoids generated (Lancaster protocol), cultured 40–50 days
- Intracerebral implantation into retrosplenial cortex of NOD-SCID immunodeficient mice
- Longitudinal analysis up to 233 dpi: IHC (SOX2, NeuN, MAP2, SMI312, GFAP), human-specific markers (hNuclei, anti-human mitochondria)
- In vivo two-photon microscopy through cranial window: GCaMP Ca2+ imaging + FITC-dextran blood flow
- Optogenetics: ChR2 in grafts, extracellular recording to detect graft-host connectivity

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 92% host survival beyond 180 dpi; 80% graft integration rate (n=28)
- Grafts survived to 233 dpi with persistent NeuN+ and SOX2+ populations
- Axonal projections confirmed to multiple host regions (corpus callosum, thalamus, etc.)
- Two-photon imaging: spontaneous Ca2+ transients, functional blood vessel formation
- Optogenetic stimulation of graft elicited responses in host neurons (synaptic connectivity)
- Integration of host microglia into grafts confirmed

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Immunodeficient mouse host required; limits immune interaction modeling
- Forebrain-dominant organoid identity (PAX6+, CTIP2+); limited regional diversity
- Chimeric model: human-mouse circuitry, not fully human
- Incomplete maturation vs. full postnatal human brain

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013: original cerebral organoid method
- Cakir et al. 2019: in vitro vascularization via ETV2 co-differentiation
- Gordon et al. 2021: long-term maturation of hCS in vitro

## 7. Glossary
- **NOD-SCID**: Nonobese diabetic severe combined immunodeficiency — immunodeficient mouse host
- **dpi**: Days post-implantation
- **VZ-L**: Ventricular zone–like region in organoid
- **Two-photon imaging**: Deep-tissue fluorescence microscopy for in vivo functional imaging
- **GCaMP**: Genetically encoded calcium indicator for neuronal activity
