## One-line Summary
Human NSC expression dynamics across corticogenesis identify cortical/NDD risk genes at critical phases; TF depletion simulations reveal spatiotemporal-dependent effects; ASD patient NSCs show recurrent TF alterations in brain patterning and lineage commitment.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Communications
- Published: 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Characterizes cortical/neuropsychiatric risk gene expression dynamics across human telencephalic fate transitions in vitro and in vivo
- Identifies brain organizers as hubs of risk gene expression; disease-specific critical phases of NSC vulnerability
- GRN simulation of TF depletion impacts across cortical fate trajectories
- Single-cell transcriptomics of ASD patient-derived NSCs reveals recurrent alterations in TFs controlling brain patterning

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Human NSC datasets across telencephalic fate transitions (in vitro + in vivo comparison)
- Risk gene expression dynamics: ASD, ID, schizophrenia, epilepsy gene sets
- GRN-based TF depletion simulation on neural cell trajectories
- scRNA-seq of ASD patient-derived NSCs (iPSC-derived)

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Risk genes enriched in brain organizers and sequential GRN programs throughout corticogenesis
- Disease-specific critical phases identified when NSCs most vulnerable
- TF depletion simulation: spatiotemporal-dependent effects on cell trajectories
- ASD patient NSCs: recurrent expression alterations in TFs for brain patterning and NSC lineage commitment

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- In vitro NSC models may not fully replicate in vivo NSC diversity
- Simulations are computational; experimental TF manipulation needed for validation
- Patient cohort size for NSC transcriptomics not specified in abstract

## 6. Related Work
- Jourdon et al. 2023: idiopathic ASD iPSC model (related patient-derived approach)
- Schafer et al. 2019: pathological priming in ASD NSCs
- Ding et al. 2026: CRISPRi screen of TFs in human RG (complementary)

## 7. Glossary
- **NSC**: Neural stem cell — here referring to iPSC-derived radial glia/neural progenitors
- **Brain organizer**: Signaling center secreting morphogens that specify regional neural identity
- **Critical phase**: Developmental window when NSCs are most vulnerable to gene dysfunction
