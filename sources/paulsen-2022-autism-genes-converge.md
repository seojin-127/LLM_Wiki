## One-line Summary
Haploinsufficiency of three top ASD risk genes (SUV420H1/KMT5B, ARID1B, CHD8) in human cortical organoids converges on asynchronous development of GABAergic and deep-layer excitatory neuron classes — distinct molecular pathways but shared cell-type vulnerability; phenotype expressivity modulated by individual genomic context.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: 10 February 2022

## 2. Key Contributions
- 745K+ cells from organoids carrying haploinsufficiency in 3 ASD genes across multiple donor lines
- Convergence: all 3 mutations cause asynchronous development of GABAergic vs. deep-layer excitatory neurons
- Distinct molecular pathways for each gene despite shared cell-type phenotype
- Individual genomic context modulates phenotype expressivity (gene×background interaction)
- Calcium imaging: early developmental changes lead to abnormal circuit activity in intact organoids

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Human cortical organoids (hCS, Pasca lab method) with CRISPR haploinsufficiency
- scRNA-seq: 745K+ cells across multiple donor cell lines per gene
- Proteomic analysis of individual organoids
- Calcium imaging in intact organoids (GCaMP)
- Multiple donors per gene: separate biological replicates

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- All 3 mutations: asynchronous GABAergic vs. deep-layer excitatory development (timing disruption)
- KMT5B, ARID1B, CHD8: each affects different molecular pathways (chromatin, BAF, Wnt, etc.)
- Phenotype consistent across cell lines but expressivity varies by donor genomic context
- Abnormal calcium imaging: downstream circuit activity disrupted from early development

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Organoid model: limited circuit complexity vs. in vivo
- Haploinsufficiency only; homozygous and de novo mutations not tested
- Donor-specific variability complicates generalization
- Mechanism of individual genomic context modulation not fully elucidated

## 6. Related Work
- Li et al. 2023 (CHOOSE): complementary pooled CRISPR screen of 36 ASD genes
- Villa et al. 2022: CHD8 in organoids (same gene, different approach)
- Jin et al. 2020: In vivo Perturb-Seq of ASD genes

## 7. Glossary
- **Asynchronous development**: Different timing of differentiation between two cell lineages
- **SUV420H1 / KMT5B**: Histone H4K20 methyltransferase; ASD risk gene
- **GABAergic neurons**: Inhibitory neurons; major interneuron type in cortex
- **hCS**: Human cortical spheroid — directed 3D cortical organoid (Pasca protocol)
