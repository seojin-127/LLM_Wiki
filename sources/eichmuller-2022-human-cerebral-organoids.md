## One-line Summary
Review covering advances in human cerebral organoid technology for modeling neurological disease, discussing how improved organoid models bridge animal models and patient studies for studying neurodevelopment, viral infections, and neurodegeneration.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Reviews Drug Discovery (or similar Nature Reviews)
- Published: 2022

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reviews cerebral organoid advances including vascularization, mixed-lineage models, microfluidics
- Discusses applications in modeling neurodevelopmental disorders, viral infections (e.g., Zika, SARS-CoV-2, EBV/MS), neurodegeneration
- Discusses translational value and current limitations
- Highlights gap between animal models and human brain due to species-specific differences

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Review article; no original experimental data

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Organoids bridge patient studies and animal models for early pathophysiology
- Vascularized organoids improve cell survival and maturation
- Key unresolved limitations: microglia, lamination, standardization

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Review article limitations apply
- Organoid field limitations: vascularization, microglia, complete lamination, scalability

## 7. Glossary
- **Cerebral organoid**: 3D self-organizing brain tissue model derived from PSCs
- **Organoid fusion**: Combining two region-specific organoids to study connectivity
