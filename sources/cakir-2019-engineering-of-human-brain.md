## One-line Summary
Engineering ETV2-expressing endothelial-like cells into human cortical organoids (hCOs) generates a functional vascular-like system with blood-brain barrier features, improving cell survival, maturation, and enabling longer-term organoid viability.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Methods
- Published: 2019

## 2. Key Contributions
- Engineered vascular-like system in hCOs by co-differentiating ETV2-expressing vascular progenitors
- Vascularized hCOs show blood-brain barrier features and improved cell survival
- Overcomes critical limitation of avascular organoids (apoptosis in inner regions)
- Improved neuronal maturation and prolonged organoid viability

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- iPSC differentiation: co-generation of neural + ETV2-expressing vascular progenitor cells
- 3D co-culture in cortical organoid conditions
- Characterization: immunostaining, scRNA-seq, BBB functional assays
- Comparison with non-vascularized hCOs

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- ETV2 cells form vascular-like network in organoids
- BBB-associated gene expression and reduced apoptosis in inner organoid regions
- Improved neuronal maturation vs non-vascularized controls
- Cell survival extended in long-term culture

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Vascular-like cells are not true brain endothelium; functional BBB incomplete
- No pericytes or astrocyte endfeet in initial model
- Metabolite/oxygen transport not fully recapitulated

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **ETV2**: ETS variant transcription factor 2 — master regulator of vascular/endothelial specification
- **hCO**: Human cortical organoid
- **BBB**: Blood-brain barrier — selectively permeable barrier between blood and brain tissue
