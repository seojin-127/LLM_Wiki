## One-line Summary
Review summarizing advances in 3D brain organoid technology including vascularized organoids, synthetic biomaterials, microfluidics, and their applications in modeling neurodevelopmental disorders, viral neuroinflammation, and neurodegeneration.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell Death Discovery
- Published: 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive review of 3D brain organoid generation methods and technology advances
- Covers vascularized organoids, mixed-lineage tissue from PSCs, synthetic biomaterials, microfluidics
- Applications: preterm birth brain dysfunction, viral infections (neuroinflammation), neurodevelopmental/neurodegenerative diseases
- Discusses translational value and current field challenges

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Review article; synthesizes published methods and findings

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Organoids recapitulate human brain structure and function better than 2D and animal models
- Advances in vascularization and microfluidics improve viability and maturation
- Translational applications emerging but scale-up challenges remain

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Review limitations; field limitations: standardization, microglia, vascularization, lamination

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013; Pasca et al. 2015; Knoblich lab advances

## 7. Glossary
- **PSC**: Pluripotent stem cell
- **Demolecularization**: Mapping molecular mechanisms of brain disease
