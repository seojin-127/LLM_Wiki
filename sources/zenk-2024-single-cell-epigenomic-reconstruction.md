## One-line Summary
Single-cell profiling of H3K27ac, H3K27me3, and H3K4me3 histone modifications from pluripotency through neuroepithelium to retinal and brain specification in human neural organoids reveals that H3K27me3 deposition at the neuroectoderm stage is required for downstream fate restriction.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Neuroscience
- Published: 2024

## 2. Key Contributions
- First single-cell multi-histone modification atlas across human neural organoid differentiation
- Tracks H3K27ac, H3K27me3, H3K4me3 from iPSC → neuroepithelium → retinal/brain progenitors
- H3K27me3 at neuroectoderm stage required for subsequent fate restriction (functional experiment)
- Epigenomic trajectories precede transcriptional identity changes
- Maps bivalent domains during neural commitment

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Single-cell CUT&Tag for H3K27ac, H3K27me3, H3K4me3 in human neural organoids
- Multiple organoid protocols capturing retinal and brain specification
- Trajectory analysis from pluripotency through lineage commitment
- Functional perturbation: EZH2 inhibition at neuroectoderm stage to block H3K27me3

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Epigenomic state transitions precede transcriptional differentiation
- H3K27me3 gain at neuroectoderm stage marks genes to be silenced in neural lineages
- EZH2 inhibition at NE stage → disrupted retinal vs. brain fate restriction
- Bivalent (H3K4me3+H3K27me3) domains resolve during neural commitment
- Retinal vs. brain epigenomic divergence detectable before transcriptional separation

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Organoid system; chromatin dynamics may not fully match in vivo trajectories
- Single-cell CUT&Tag coverage lower than bulk; rare chromatin states may be missed
- Limited to three histone marks; other modifications (H3K36me3, H3K9me3) not profiled

## 7. Glossary
- **CUT&Tag**: Cleavage under targets and tagmentation — antibody-based chromatin profiling compatible with single cells
- **H3K27me3**: Repressive histone mark deposited by PRC2/EZH2; marks silenced regions
- **H3K27ac**: Active enhancer/promoter mark; mutually exclusive with H3K27me3
- **H3K4me3**: Active promoter mark; bivalent when co-occurring with H3K27me3
- **EZH2**: Histone methyltransferase; writes H3K27me3; component of PRC2 complex
- **Bivalent domain**: Chromatin region with both H3K4me3 (active) and H3K27me3 (repressive); poised for activation or silencing
