## One-line Summary
Science review mapping the molecular and cellular mechanisms underlying cortical malformations (microcephaly, lissencephaly, polymicrogyria, dysplasia, heterotopias); integrates genetics, progenitor biology, and organoid/mouse models to explain how disrupted development leads to structural cortical abnormalities.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Science
- Published: 2022

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comprehensive review of cortical malformation genetics and cell biology
- Categorizes malformations: microcephaly, lissencephaly, polymicrogyria, focal cortical dysplasia, heterotopias
- Reviews molecular pathways disrupted in each malformation type (progenitor proliferation, migration, lamination)
- Integrates organoid and mouse model findings with human genetic data
- Discusses convergence of diverse mutations on shared cellular vulnerability points

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Review article; no primary experimental data

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Progenitor over-proliferation, under-proliferation, or abnormal differentiation → distinct malformation types
- Migration defects → heterotopias, lissencephaly
- Post-mitotic lamination defects → polymicrogyria, dysplasia
- Many causal genes converge on centrosome function (microcephaly), actin/tubulin dynamics (lissencephaly), mTOR signaling (focal dysplasia)

## 7. Glossary
- **Microcephaly**: Reduced brain size due to progenitor proliferation defects
- **Lissencephaly**: Loss of cortical folding; caused by neuronal migration defects
- **Polymicrogyria**: Numerous small, irregular cortical folds
- **Heterotopia**: Mispositioned neurons due to migration arrest
- **mTOR**: Mammalian target of rapamycin; signaling pathway mutated in focal cortical dysplasia
