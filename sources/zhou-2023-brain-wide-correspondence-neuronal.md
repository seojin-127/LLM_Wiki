## One-line Summary
Epi-retro-seq links single-cell epigenomes to long-distance projections for 33,034 neurons across 32 source regions projecting to 24 targets in mouse brain; integrated into BRAIN Initiative Cell Census Network atlas.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: December 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- epi-retro-seq: retrograde tracing + single-cell epigenomics to link cell type to projection target
- 33,034 neurons from 32 source regions to 24 spinal/brain targets (225 combinations)
- Systematic statistical comparisons of projection discriminability across all source-to-target pairs
- Integration with BRAIN Initiative Cell Census Network (BICCN) atlas
- Spatial transcriptomics further assigns projection-enriched clusters to sub-source regions

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Epi-retro-seq: retrograde viral injection + single-cell methylome or chromatin profiling
- 32 source regions × 24 target sites = 225 source-to-target combinations
- Integration with millions of BICCN cells for projection-to-cluster mapping
- Spatial transcriptomics integration for sub-regional source mapping

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 926 statistical comparisons of projection discriminability
- Epigenomic cell types predictive of projection target identity
- Hypothalamus, thalamus, hindbrain, amygdala, midbrain projection neurons analyzed in depth
- In-depth analysis of differentially expressed genes and cis-regulatory elements for each projection type

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Mouse only; human projection neuron epigenomics lacking
- Retrograde viral efficiency varies by region
- Brain-to-brain projections; spinal projections covered by Winter et al.

## 6. Related Work
- Winter et al. 2023: transcriptomic taxonomy of brain-wide SPNs (complementary)
- Yao et al. 2023: Allen Brain Cell Atlas (BICCN companion)
- Liu et al. 2023: single-cell DNA methylome atlas (complementary epigenomics)

## 7. Glossary
- **Epi-retro-seq**: Retrograde viral tracing combined with single-cell epigenomics (methylome or ATAC)
- **BICCN**: BRAIN Initiative Cell Census Network — consortium mouse brain atlas
- **Projection discriminability**: Statistical measure of how well epigenomic profiles distinguish neurons by target
