## One-line Summary
Forebrain organoids from 13 father-son pairs (idiopathic ASD boys vs. unaffected fathers) reveal opposite disruptions of the balance between dorsal cortical plate excitatory neurons and preplate-like neurons in macrocephalic vs. normocephalic ASD probands, driven by divergent TF expression during early cortical development.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Neuroscience
- Published: 10 August 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- First organoid study of idiopathic (not monogenic) ASD using patient-father pairs (n=13 families)
- Macrocephalic vs. normocephalic ASD: opposite imbalance of excitatory neuron subtypes (dorsal CP vs. preplate-like)
- Imbalance driven by divergent TF expression during early cortical neurogenesis
- No genomic variants explain transcriptomic alterations; but ASD rare-variant genes overlap with altered transcripts
- Suggests gene convergence between rare-variant ASD and idiopathic ASD developmental transcriptome

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Forebrain organoids from iPSCs of 13 ASD probands (boys) + their unaffected fathers
- scRNA-seq of organoids: cell type composition and transcriptomic analysis
- Comparison: macrocephalic vs. normocephalic proband subgroups
- Rare variant enrichment analysis: ASD risk gene overlap with altered transcripts

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Macrocephalic ASD: excess dorsal CP neurons, fewer preplate-like
- Normocephalic ASD: opposite imbalance (fewer CP neurons, more preplate-like)
- TF divergence at early neurogenesis drives lineage imbalance
- No genomic variant explains transcriptomic differences; but rare-variant ASD genes enriched in altered transcripts

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Forebrain organoids: limited cell type diversity; no interneurons
- 13 families: modest sample size for idiopathic ASD (heterogeneous condition)
- Father as control is unaffected but shares ~50% genetics — not ideal control

## 6. Related Work
- Paulsen et al. 2022: monogenic ASD convergence in organoids
- Schafer et al. 2019: idiopathic ASD NSC progenitor dysregulation
- Jin et al. 2020: ASD risk gene modules (in vivo)

## 7. Glossary
- **Idiopathic ASD**: ASD without identified single-gene cause; polygenic/complex
- **Macrocephaly**: Larger than average head circumference; ~25% of ASD probands
- **Dorsal cortical plate (CP)**: Main excitatory neuron layer of developing cortex
- **Preplate**: Earliest cortical neurons; displaced by CP expansion (Cajal-Retzius, subplate neurons)
- **TF divergence**: Different transcription factor expression levels driving altered lineage specification
