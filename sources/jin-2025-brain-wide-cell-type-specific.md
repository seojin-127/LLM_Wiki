## One-line Summary
~1.2M single-cell transcriptomes from young adult and aged mice (both sexes, brain-wide) yield 847 cell clusters; 14 age-biased clusters (mostly glial) and 2,449 unique age-DE genes; hypothalamic third-ventricle region identified as aging hub.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: February 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- ~1.2M high-quality cells from young adult + aged mice, both sexes, forebrain + midbrain + hindbrain
- 847 cell clusters; 14 age-biased clusters (mostly glial)
- 2,449 unique differentially expressed genes (age-DE genes) across neuronal and non-neuronal types
- Common aging signatures: decreased neuronal structure/function genes in neurons, astrocytes, oligodendrocytes; increased immune/inflammation in immune and vascular cells
- Third ventricle hypothalamic region identified as aging hub: tanycytes, ependymal cells, arcuate/DMH/PVN neurons most sensitive

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- snRNA-seq of dissected brain regions from young adult and aged mice of both sexes
- Brain regions spanning forebrain, midbrain, hindbrain
- High-resolution clustering: 847 cell clusters across all brain-wide cell types

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 14 age-biased clusters: mostly glial types with enriched or depleted abundance
- Neuron subtypes with greatest age-DE enrichment: tanycytes, ependymal cells, arcuate/DMH/PVN neurons
- Common pattern: neurons age → decreased neuronal function genes; immune cells → increased inflammation genes
- Third ventricle hypothalamus: dual decrease in neuronal function + increase in immune response → aging hub

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Mouse only; human aging atlas lacking
- Cross-sectional (young vs. old); longitudinal tracking not possible
- Cell-type-specific mechanisms of aging not experimentally validated

## 6. Related Work
- Yao et al. 2023: Allen Brain Cell Atlas (reference atlas for cell type integration)
- Van Velthoven et al. 2025: telencephalic GABAergic taxonomy (related atlas effort)

## 7. Glossary
- **Age-DE genes**: Differentially expressed between young adult and aged mice in a cell-type-specific manner
- **Tanycytes**: Specialized ependymal cells of the third ventricle; regulate energy homeostasis
- **Arcuate nucleus / DMH / PVN**: Hypothalamic nuclei involved in energy homeostasis and aging
