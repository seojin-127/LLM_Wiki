## One-line Summary
Patient-derived forebrain organoids from individuals with homozygous CNTNAP2 truncating mutations show cortical overgrowth driven by increased neural progenitor proliferation; CRISPR correction rescues the phenotype.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Communications
- Published: 2021

## 2. Key Contributions
- First forebrain organoid model of CNTNAP2-associated ASD with clinical brain overgrowth
- Patient iPSC organoids show increased volume and cell number driven by progenitor proliferation
- scRNA-seq identifies PFC excitatory neurons as key CNTNAP2-expressing cell types
- DEGs enriched for ASD-associated genes and clinical phenotypes of CNTNAP2 patients
- CRISPR-Cas9 mutation correction rescues overgrowth phenotype

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- hiPSC from CNTNAP2 homozygous truncating mutation patients
- Forebrain organoid generation + volumetric and cell number quantification
- Single-cell RNA-seq for cell type characterization and DEG analysis
- CRISPR-Cas9 correction of CNTNAP2 mutation as rescue experiment

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Patient organoids show increased volume and cell number vs. controls
- Increased progenitor proliferation drives overgrowth
- scRNA-seq: PFC excitatory neurons highest CNTNAP2 expression
- DEGs enriched for ASD risk genes and CNTNAP2 patient clinical features
- CRISPR correction → normalized organoid size

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Homozygous truncating mutation (rare syndromic ASD); may not generalize to idiopathic ASD
- Organoid model lacks full cortical architecture and connectivity
- Mechanistic link between CNTNAP2 loss and progenitor proliferation not fully established

## 6. Related Work
- Li et al. 2023 (CHOOSE): CRISPR screen of ASD risk genes in organoids
- Paulsen et al. 2022: ASD gene convergence on neuronal development in organoids
- Villa et al. 2022: CHD8 haploinsufficiency organoid model

## 7. Glossary
- **CNTNAP2**: Contactin-associated protein-like 2; large synaptic protein; mutations cause Cortical Dysplasia-Focal Epilepsy (CDFE) syndrome and ASD
- **Cortical overgrowth**: Macrocephaly phenotype associated with subset of ASD patients
