## One-line Summary
Cross-species single-cell transcriptomic atlas of the vertebrate retina from 17 species reveals conservation of cell classes and subclasses, with increasing divergence toward retinal output neurons (RGCs), and identifies rodent orthologues of primate midget RGCs.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Received: 30 March 2023
- Published: 13 December 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- Generated and integrated scRNA-seq/snRNA-seq atlases of the retina from 17 vertebrate species (humans, 2 NHP, 4 rodents, 3 ungulates, opossum, ferret, tree shrew, bird, reptile, teleost fish, lamprey)
- Found high molecular conservation of 6 retinal cell classes (photoreceptors, horizontal cells, bipolar cells, amacrine cells, RGCs, Müller glia); transcriptomic variation scales with evolutionary distance
- Variation among cell types increases from outer retina (photoreceptors) to inner retina (RGCs), suggesting evolution preferentially shapes retinal output
- Identified mouse orthologues of midget RGCs — previously believed primate-specific; mouse orthologues have large receptive fields and comprise ~2% of mouse RGCs vs >80% in humans

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- scRNA-seq and snRNA-seq across 17 species; data integrated using cross-species alignment methods
- Transcriptomic conservation analyzed relative to phylogenetic distance
- Cell type matching across species using conserved gene expression programs
- Projection analysis of RGC subtypes to thalamus and primary visual cortex

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 6 retinal cell classes conserved across all 17 species at molecular level
- Major subclasses also conserved; cell type variation more pronounced within subclasses
- Many cell types shared across species based on conserved gene expression programs tracing to early ancestral vertebrate
- Midget RGCs: >80% of human RGCs; mouse orthologues ~2% with large receptive fields
- Both primate and mouse midget RGC orthologues overrepresented in thalamus (→ primary visual cortex)

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Single-cell resolution limited by tissue dissociation; rare cell types may be underrepresented
- Functional validation of cross-species cell type homologies needed
- Lamprey as outgroup provides deep evolutionary context but genome annotation is less complete

## 6. Related Work
- Cross-species brain cell atlas studies (Allen Brain Atlas, BICCN)
- Midget RGC biology in primates (previous work assumed primate-restricted)
- scRNA-seq integration methods (Seurat, Harmony, scVI)

## 7. Glossary
- **RGC**: Retinal ganglion cell — the output neurons of the retina projecting to brain
- **Midget RGC**: Dominant RGC subtype in primates, subserves high-acuity vision; projects to parvocellular LGN
- **Müller glia**: Radial glia of the retina; major glial cell class
- **snRNA-seq**: Single-nucleus RNA sequencing, used for frozen/hard-to-dissociate tissues
