## One-line Summary
Nature Protocols paper describing the generation of human telencephalic organoids from single isolated neural rosettes; produces organoids with reproducible cellular composition, predictable organization, and functional neural networks; includes SHANK3 deletion disease modeling.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Protocols
- Published: February 2026

## 2. Key Contributions
- Protocol for generating telencephalic brain organoids from single, isolated neural rosettes (not clusters)
- Single rosette origin mimics development from singular neural tube in vivo
- More reproducible cellular composition and predictable spatial organization vs. multi-rosette organoids
- Functionally mature neurons: action potentials, excitatory and inhibitory synaptic inputs
- CRISPR-Cas9 protocol for hemizygous SHANK3 deletion as ASD disease model
- Guidance on scRNA-seq, immunohistochemistry, and slice patch-clamp electrophysiology

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- CRISPR-Cas9 engineering of iPSCs (SHANK3 hemizygous deletion)
- Neural rosette isolation from stem cell cultures
- Organoid generation from single rosette → telencephalic patterning
- Electrophysiology: slice patch-clamp to confirm functional neural networks
- scRNA-seq readout for cellular composition characterization

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Single rosette-derived organoids: predictable cellular organization around single lumen
- Functional neurons: generate action potentials, receive synaptic inputs
- SHANK3 deletion organoids: model for ASD synaptic pathology
- Protocol duration: 5–6 months from stem cells to characterized organoid

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Long protocol duration (5–6 months)
- Requires cell culture expertise
- Single rosette isolation labor-intensive
- Applications described as biocomputer development (speculative)

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013: cerebral organoid foundational protocol
- Revah et al. 2022: organoid maturation and functional integration
- Wang et al. 2022 (Nat Commun): companion paper from Shcheglovitov lab

## 7. Glossary
- **Neural rosette**: Radially organized neural progenitor structure in vitro; models neural tube formation
- **SHANK3**: Postsynaptic scaffolding protein; haploinsufficiency causes Phelan-McDermid syndrome (ASD)
- **Telencephalic**: Referring to the telencephalon (cerebrum + basal ganglia)
- **Slice patch-clamp**: Electrophysiological recording from neurons in tissue slices
