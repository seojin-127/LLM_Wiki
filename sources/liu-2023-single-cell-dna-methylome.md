## One-line Summary
Brain-wide single-cell DNA methylome (snmC-seq3) and 3D multi-omic (snm3C-seq: methylome + chromatin conformation) atlas of adult mouse brain from 117 anatomical regions, defining 4,673 cell groups and 274 subclasses, identifying 2.6M differentially methylated regions, and linking spatial methylation patterns to transcription.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: 14 December 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- First whole-brain single-cell DNA methylation atlas: 301,626 methylomes from 117 dissected regions
- Joint methylome + 3D chromatin conformation (snm3C-seq): 176,003 profiles — first brain-wide 3D multi-omic atlas
- 4,673 cell groups and 274 cross-modality-annotated subclasses (methylation-based taxonomy)
- 2.6 million differentially methylated regions (potential gene regulatory elements)
- Spatial cytosine methylation patterns in cell types within and across brain regions
- Regulatory networks: TF → CRE → target gene incorporating all three modalities
- Intragenic methylation + chromatin conformation predict alternative isoform expression

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- snmC-seq3: single-nucleus methylcytosine sequencing (3rd generation, high sensitivity)
- snm3C-seq: joint single-nucleus methylation + Hi-C for chromatin conformation
- 117 anatomical dissectates across whole adult mouse brain (55 mice)
- Integration with companion transcriptome (snRNA-seq) and chromatin accessibility (snATAC-seq) datasets
- Brain-wide spatial transcriptomics for validation of spatial epigenetic diversity

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 4,673 methylation-based cell groups → 274 subclasses aligned with transcriptomic taxonomy
- 2.6M differentially methylated regions across genome
- Spatial methylation patterns: cell-type-specific AND anatomical region-specific
- Chromatin conformation diversities: highest in neuronal genes; correlated with methylation and transcription
- Alternative isoform prediction: intragenic methylation + Hi-C predicts SMART-seq2-observed isoforms
- Multi-omic regulatory networks: TF binding sites → CREs → downstream targets integrated

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Adult brain only; developmental methylation dynamics not captured
- snmC-seq3 and snm3C-seq have lower cell recovery than snRNA-seq
- 3D conformation data sparse at single-cell level; statistical uncertainty in Hi-C-based contacts

## 6. Related Work
- Zemke et al. 2023: cross-species multiomics in motor cortex (related multi-omic approach)
- Langlieb et al. 2023: same whole-brain adult mouse atlas (transcriptomic)
- Liu et al. 2025 (human-specific enhancer): CRE functional biology

## 7. Glossary
- **snmC-seq3**: Single-nucleus methylcytosine sequencing (3rd generation)
- **snm3C-seq**: Single-nucleus methylation + Hi-C — joint 3D conformation + methylation
- **mCG/mCH**: CpG methylation / non-CpG methylation (both measured)
- **DMR**: Differentially methylated region — potential regulatory element
- **Alternative isoform**: Spliced mRNA variant; predicted here from intragenic methylation patterns
