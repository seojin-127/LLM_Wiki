## One-line Summary
Single-cell long-read RNA-seq atlas of mouse and human brain reveals that 72% of genes show full-length isoform variation across cell types, regions, developmental stages, or species — with cell-type-specific splicing being strongest in the adolescent transition and partially conserved from mouse to human hippocampus.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Neuroscience
- Published: June 2024

## 2. Key Contributions
- First comprehensive single-cell long-read isoform atlas of mouse and human brain
- 72% of genes show isoform variation across at least one axis (cell type, region, development, species)
- Splicing, transcription start sites, and polyadenylation sites all vary strongly between cell types
- Adolescent transition (P21→P28): strongest developmental isoform regulation
- Mouse-to-human conservation: cell-type-specific isoform patterns mostly maintained in hippocampus; human has additional gain-of-function isoforms
- Disease-associated variation linked to cell-type-specific isoforms

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Single-cell long-read sequencing (PacBio Iso-Seq / ONT) across mouse brain regions, developmental time points
- Human hippocampus for cross-species comparison
- Isoform mapping: full-length transcripts per cell type
- Developmental analysis: multiple time points from embryonic to adult
- Disease variant mapping to isoform-specific regulatory elements

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 72% isoform variable genes across cell type/region/development/species axes
- Splicing + TSS + polyA variation strongest between cell types
- Neurotransmitter transport and synapse turnover genes: cell-type-specific isoform variability across regions
- P21→P28: strongest developmental splicing regulation (adolescent window)
- Developmental > regional isoform regulation for same cell type
- Human brain: additional cell-type-specific isoforms (gain-of-function vs. mouse)

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Long-read coverage per cell lower than short-read; rare isoforms may be missed
- Not all brain regions or developmental stages covered
- Human data limited to hippocampus for cross-species comparison

## 6. Related Work
- Pertea et al.: transcript isoform annotation tools
- Foord et al. 2025: spatial long-read approach (complementary spatial context)
- Allen Brain Atlas: reference for brain region definitions

## 7. Glossary
- **Iso-Seq**: PacBio long-read sequencing method for full-length isoform sequencing
- **TSS**: Transcription start site — alternative TSS usage is a major source of isoform diversity
- **PolyA**: Polyadenylation site — 3' end variation; affects mRNA stability and translation
- **Adolescent transition (P21→P28)**: Mouse developmental window of visual system maturation and critical period
