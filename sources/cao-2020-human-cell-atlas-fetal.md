## One-line Summary
sci-RNA-seq3 applied to 121 human fetal samples (72–129 days, 15 organs) generates a 4-million-cell atlas with 657 cell subtypes, providing a comprehensive cross-tissue reference for human fetal development and enabling discovery of circulating trophoblast-like and hepatoblast-like cells in unexpected tissues.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Science
- Published: 2020

## 2. Key Contributions
- First comprehensive multi-organ human fetal cell atlas: 4 million single cells, 121 samples, 15 organs, 72–129 days post-conception
- 657 cell subtypes identified and preliminarily annotated (cross-matched to mouse atlases)
- Circulating trophoblast-like and hepatoblast-like cells found in unexpected tissues (highlights cross-tissue trafficking)
- Multi-organ map of blood cell state trajectories from HSCs to all major sublineages
- Companion to Domcke et al. (chromatin accessibility atlas, same issue)
- Quantitative framework for cell type specificity assessment

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- sci-RNA-seq3 (three-level single-cell combinatorial indexing): highly scalable, millions of cells per experiment
- 121 fetal samples, 15 organs: adrenal gland, cerebellum, cerebrum, heart, intestine, kidney, liver, lung, muscle, pancreas, placenta, skin, spleen, thymus, trachea
- Cross-matching to mouse cell atlases for preliminary annotation
- Cell type specificity framework developed

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 4M cells → 657 cell subtypes across 15 organs
- Circulating trophoblast-like cells: detected in unexpected organs (blood)
- Blood cell trajectory map: HSC → all sublineages across organs
- Transcriptomic cell type classification broadly concordant across organs for shared types (endothelial, blood, epithelial)

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Preliminary annotation (cross-matched to mouse); human-specific cell types may be mislabeled
- sci-RNA-seq3: lower sensitivity per cell vs. droplet methods
- Not all organs equally represented; depth varies
- Chromatin accessibility companion (Domcke et al.) needed for regulatory interpretation

## 6. Related Work
- Domcke et al. 2020: companion chromatin accessibility atlas (same Science issue)
- Herring et al. 2022: human PFC development (postnatal complement)
- Braun et al. 2023: first-trimester brain atlas (brain-focused)

## 7. Glossary
- **sci-RNA-seq3**: Three-level single-cell combinatorial indexing RNA-seq — massively scalable
- **HSC**: Hematopoietic stem cell
- **Trophoblast**: Placenta-forming extraembryonic cell; circulating forms unexpected finding
- **Cell type specificity**: Degree to which gene expression characterizes a unique cell type vs. shared
