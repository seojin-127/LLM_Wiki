## One-line Summary
Chronic glucocorticoid (GC) exposure during neural organoid differentiation shifts lineage specification toward inhibitory neurons via PBX3 and other TFs, with lasting cell-type-specific changes overlapping ASD risk genes — demonstrating convergence of genetic and environmental neurodevelopmental risk factors.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Science Advances
- Published: 14 February 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- First single-cell characterization of GC effects on human neurodevelopmental lineage specification in organoids
- Chronic GC exposure biases cell fate toward inhibitory neuron lineage via PBX3 transcription factor
- Lasting cell-type-specific transcriptional changes include ASD risk genes
- Convergence of genetic (ASD risk genes) and environmental (GC) risk factors via shared lineage specification mechanism
- Integration of scRNA-seq + chromatin accessibility (scATAC-seq) to characterize GRNs

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Unguided human neural organoids (hPSC-derived)
- Chronic GC (dexamethasone) treatment during differentiation
- Single-cell RNA-seq + scATAC-seq for GRN characterization
- Cell-type-specific analysis; comparison to control organoids
- ASD risk gene overlap analysis

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Chronic GC: amplified inhibitory neuron lineage commitment (primed by PBX3 TF)
- Cell-type-specific lasting changes: includes genes associated with ASD risk
- GRN analysis: GC-responsive TFs linked to chromatin accessibility changes in inhibitory neuron progenitors
- Convergence hypothesis: GC exposure → similar pathway perturbations as ASD genetic variants

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Unguided organoid model; limited spatial/regional resolution
- Chronic pharmacological GC (dexamethasone) vs. endogenous cortisol — dose/timing questions
- Mechanistic link between PBX3 and downstream inhibitory fate requires further validation

## 7. Glossary
- **GC**: Glucocorticoid — steroid hormone (cortisol/dexamethasone); antenatal exposure is psychiatric risk factor
- **PBX3**: Pre-B cell leukemia homeobox 3 — TF identified as GC-responsive driver of inhibitory fate
- **E/I balance**: Excitatory/inhibitory balance — critical for proper brain circuit function; disrupted in ASD/schizophrenia
- **Lineage specification**: Process by which progenitor cells commit to particular cell fates
