## One-line Summary
Review proposing a framework for linking rare protein-disrupting variants in profound autism to cell-type function using brain transcriptomic atlases and foundation models; addresses the challenge of broad gene expression vs. cell-type-specific vulnerability; proposes combining perturbations, AI inference, and postmortem validation.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell Genomics
- Published: 2026

## 2. Key Contributions
- Framework for quantifying trade-off between a gene's cell-type specificity and sensitivity in ASD
- Reviews how brain transcriptomic atlases decode cellular consequences of rare gene-disrupting variants
- Proposes foundation models (linking genetic variation to cell-type transcriptomes) to clarify cellular functions affected by autism variants
- Addresses limited overlap between genetic associations and transcriptomic alterations in autistic brains
- Unifying mechanistic framework: experimental perturbation + AI inference + postmortem validation

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Review/perspective article; no primary experimental data
- Synthesizes literature on: rare variant genetics, single-cell brain atlases, foundation models for perturbation prediction

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Identifies challenge: broad expression of ASD genes confounds cell-type-specific effects
- Proposes cell-type specificity × sensitivity framework to prioritize vulnerable cell types
- Foundation models (e.g., perturbation prediction FMs) could clarify variant → cell-type → phenotype links
- Postmortem validation essential to confirm AI-driven predictions

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Review/framework paper; experimental validation pending
- Foundation model predictions for rare variants still limited by training data distribution

## 6. Related Work
- jin-2020 (in vivo Perturb-Seq): ASD gene perturbation in mouse cortex
- paulsen-2022 (ASD genes converge): organoid-based ASD gene convergence
- li-2023 (CHOOSE): pooled CRISPR screen for ASD genes in organoids

## 7. Glossary
- **Profound autism**: Severe ASD with intellectual disability; distinct genetic architecture from high-functioning ASD
- **Cell-type specificity**: Degree to which a gene is expressed preferentially in specific cell types
- **Rare variant**: Low-frequency protein-disrupting variant; often de novo in ASD
- **Foundation model perturbation prediction**: Using pretrained single-cell FM to predict gene KO effects
