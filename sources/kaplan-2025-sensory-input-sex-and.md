## One-line Summary
Paired transcriptomic and chromatin accessibility profiling of the hypothalamic preoptic region across postnatal development reveals that neuronal developmental trajectories are shaped by sex, sensory input (especially vomeronasal), and cell function, with distinct stages including early diversification, sex difference emergence, and weaning/puberty transitions.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: 6 November 2025 (Vol. 647)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Mapped developmental trajectories of preoptic area (POA) neuronal populations using paired scRNA-seq + scATAC-seq across postnatal stages
- Identified four key developmental stages: early diversification, perinatal sex difference emergence, postnatal maturation/signaling refinement, weaning/puberty nonlinear transitions
- Sex of animal is a major shaper of developmental trajectories; sex differences emerge perinatally
- Vomeronasal sensing plays a major role in timing of preoptic cell type maturation (assessed in sensory mutants)
- Cell type developmental trajectories depend on anatomical location and functional role

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Paired single-cell RNA-seq + single-cell ATAC-seq of hypothalamic preoptic region across postnatal development
- Male vs female comparisons at multiple time points
- Vomeronasal sensory mutant mice used to assess sensory input role
- Chromatin accessibility dynamics profiled to identify regulatory changes

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Marked diversity of developmental trajectories across POA neuronal populations
- Sex differences in transcription emerge perinatally, before puberty
- Vomeronasal input required for normal timing of preoptic maturation
- Nonlinear transcriptional changes accelerate at weaning and puberty
- Location and function (homeostatic vs behavioral) predict trajectory shape

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Limited to mouse model; human hypothalamic development may differ
- Spatial resolution limited without spatial transcriptomics validation
- Functional circuit validation needed for identified populations

## 7. Glossary
- **POA**: Preoptic area — hypothalamic region controlling thermoregulation, sleep, reproductive behavior
- **Vomeronasal organ**: Accessory olfactory organ detecting pheromones; key for social/reproductive behavior in mice
- **scATAC-seq**: Single-cell ATAC-seq — chromatin accessibility at single-cell resolution
