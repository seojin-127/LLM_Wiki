## One-line Summary
DBiT-based spatial tri-omic atlas (ATAC+RNA+protein and CUT&Tag+RNA+protein) of mouse brain P0-P21 plus neuroinflammation model reveals dynamic chromatin priming of layer-defining TFs and shared developmental-inflammatory molecular programs.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature
- Published: November 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Spatial ATAC-RNA-protein (ARP-seq) and spatial CUT&Tag-RNA-protein (CTRP-seq) applied to same tissue section
- CODEX multiplexed immunofluorescence (23-plex mouse, 19-plex human) for spatial cell type mapping
- Spatiotemporal tri-omic atlas from P0 to P21 mouse brain + comparison to human developing brain
- Identifies temporal persistence and spatial spreading of chromatin accessibility for layer-defining TFs
- Dynamic chromatin priming of myelin genes across corpus callosum subregions
- Neuroinflammation (lysolecithin) model: microglia activation programs shared with development

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- DBiT (deterministic barcoding in tissue): enables simultaneous spatial profiling of multiple omics layers
- Spatial ARP-seq: ATAC + RNA + protein from same tissue section
- Spatial CTRP-seq: CUT&Tag (H3K27me3) + RNA + protein from same tissue section
- CODEX multiplexed immunofluorescence imaging
- P0-P21 mouse brain developmental time series + human second-trimester comparison
- Lysolecithin (LPC) focal demyelination model for neuroinflammation

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Layer-defining TF chromatin accessibility persists and spreads spatially across P0-P21
- Corpus callosum: chromatin priming of myelin genes precedes myelination; projection neurons coordinate axonogenesis
- Neuroinflammation: microglia show conserved and distinct programs; distal activation observed
- Common molecular programs between brain development and neuroinflammation resolution

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Mouse P0-P21 window; human data limited to second-trimester for comparison
- DBiT spatial resolution ~10 µm per pixel; near but not single-cell resolution
- Neuroinflammation model (LPC) may not fully capture human MS pathology

## 6. Related Work

## 7. Glossary
- **DBiT**: Deterministic barcoding in tissue — spatial multi-omics platform by Rong Fan lab
- **ARP-seq**: ATAC-RNA-protein sequencing — simultaneous spatial profiling
- **CTRP-seq**: CUT&Tag-RNA-protein sequencing — histone mark + transcriptome + protein
- **CODEX**: CO-Detection by indEXing — highly multiplexed immunofluorescence imaging
- **LPC (lysolecithin)**: Focal demyelination agent used to model neuroinflammation/MS
