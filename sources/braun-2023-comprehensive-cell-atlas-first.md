## One-line Summary
BICCN first-trimester human brain atlas: 1.67M cells from 26 brain specimens (5–14 pcw), 616 clusters; reveals RG-to-neuron differentiation programs, GABAergic migration from MGE to thalamus, regional glioblast diversity, and early OPC specification.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Science
- Published: 2023

## 2. Key Contributions
- First comprehensive whole-brain cell atlas of the first trimester (5–14 pcw)
- 1,665,937 high-quality single-cell transcriptomes from 26 specimens dissected into 111 samples
- 616 clusters with metadata: class, subclass, spatial location, age, marker genes
- Spatial transcriptomics at 5 pcw (FISH + spatial RNA-seq)
- Reveals: three concurrent neocortical programs (differentiation, cell cycle, maturation); IPC progenitor state balance; CRABP+ MGE→thalamus GABAergic migration; regional glioblast/astrocyte specification; early OPC regionalization

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- sci-RNA-seq3 on 111 biological samples from 26 brain specimens (5–14 pcw)
- Spatial: multiplexed RNA FISH + Visium spatial transcriptomics at 5 pcw
- Clustering: 616 clusters annotated with class/subclass/spatial/age metadata

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- 1.67M cells; 616 clusters = most comprehensive first-trimester brain atlas
- Neocortical excitatory lineage: 3 concurrent programs (differentiation/cell cycle/maturation)
- IPCs: delicate balance of progenitor vs. neurogenic TFs; neurogenic induction after G1
- MGE CRABP+ cells migrate to thalamus → predict adult PVALB+ thalamic neurons
- Ventral midbrain: diverse progenitors at 8 pcw; TH adult subtypes arise after 14 pcw
- Glioblasts: regionally specific; most become region-specific astrocytes
- OPCs: forebrain/midbrain/hindbrain-specific with conserved functional genes

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- First trimester only (5–14 pcw); second trimester onward covered by other atlases
- Spatial resolution: Visium (55 µm spots) for spatial validation
- Human embryonic tissue is rare/limited in cell numbers per stage

## 6. Related Work
- Mannens et al. 2025: chromatin accessibility in first trimester (same developmental window, different modality)
- Zeng et al. 2023: even earlier (gastrulation, CS7-13)
- Cao et al. 2020: multi-organ fetal atlas (different scope)

## 7. Glossary
- **pcw**: Post-conceptional weeks; fetal staging convention
- **IPC**: Intermediate progenitor cell — divides to amplify neuronal output
- **CRABP**: Cellular retinoic acid-binding protein — marker for migrating GABAergic neurons
- **OPC**: Oligodendrocyte precursor cell — early glial lineage
