## One-line Summary
Human-macaque comparative single-cell atlas (snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq + spatial) of postnatal PFC development reveals human-specific prolonged maturation via glial progenitor proliferation, key synaptogenesis/pruning windows, and cell types most vulnerable to neurodevelopmental disorders.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Neuroscience
- Published: 2025

## 2. Key Contributions
- Comparative human vs. macaque postnatal PFC atlas: snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq + spatial transcriptomics
- Species-specific dynamic trajectories of cell types across postnatal development
- Human-specific prolonged PFC maturation: glial progenitors show higher proliferation in humans vs. macaques
- Key developmental windows for synaptogenesis, synaptic pruning, and gliogenesis
- Cell types and lineages most vulnerable to neurodevelopmental/psychiatric disorders
- TFs with human-specific expression features identified

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- 38 human neocortical samples + matched macaque samples
- snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq (paired multiome) + spatial transcriptomics
- Spans first trimester to adolescence in humans; equivalent macaque stages
- Integration for cross-species comparison of cell type trajectories

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Human PFC maturation: more prolonged than macaque; glial progenitors more proliferative
- Synaptogenesis/pruning windows: identified key postnatal stages
- Gliogenesis: similar timing but human-specific transcriptional features
- Neurodevelopmental/psychiatric disorder risk: enriched in specific cell types and lineages
- Human-specific TF expression: regulators of extended maturation programs

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Postnatal focus; prenatal stages not covered in this study
- Macaque comparison limited by availability of matched samples
- Spatial resolution: standard Visium or similar

## 6. Related Work
- Herring et al. 2022: human PFC snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq from gestation to adulthood
- Kanton et al. 2019: cross-species organoid comparison (human-specific neoteny)
- Wang et al. 2025: developing human neocortex atlas (complementary)

## 7. Glossary
- **Postnatal PFC**: Prefrontal cortex development from birth through adolescence
- **Synaptogenesis**: Formation of synaptic connections; peak timing varies by layer/cell type
- **Synaptic pruning**: Activity-dependent elimination of synaptic connections; key in adolescence
