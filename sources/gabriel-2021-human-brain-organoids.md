## One-line Summary
iPSC-derived brain organoids (OVB-organoids) self-organize bilaterally symmetric optic vesicles from the forebrain-like region without artificial signaling centers; these optic structures contain light-sensitive retinal cell types, enabling inter-organ interaction studies within a single organoid.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Cell Stem Cell
- Published: 7 October 2021

## 2. Key Contributions
- First demonstration of bilateral optic vesicle formation in brain organoids (self-organized, no extrinsic retinal patterning needed)
- OVB-organoids (optic vesicle-bearing organoids) contain neural (retinal ganglion-like, photoreceptor-like) and non-neural (RPE-like) cell types
- Bilateral symmetry: recapitulates development of paired eye fields from anterior brain
- Functional light sensitivity: OVB-organoids respond to light stimulation
- Platform for inter-organ interaction within single organoid

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Standard iPSC-derived unguided brain organoid protocol
- No artificial retinal patterning signals required (intrinsic self-organization)
- Characterization: IHC for retinal markers (CHX10, OTX2, MITF, PROX1, etc.)
- scRNA-seq: retinal cell type identification
- Calcium imaging for light sensitivity
- Bilateral symmetry confirmed by 3D imaging

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Bilateral symmetric optic vesicles: ~15–30% of organoids form optic structures
- Retinal cell types: photoreceptor precursors, RPE-like, retinal ganglion-like cells
- Temporal development: optic vesicles emerge from week 4–5 onward
- Light response: calcium imaging demonstrates photosensitivity
- Brain-retina connectivity within same organoid

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Low efficiency (~15–30%); variable and unpredictable formation
- Immature retinal cell types; incomplete photoreceptor maturation
- No vascularization; limited tissue depth

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013: original cerebral organoid
- Pasca et al. 2015: hCS method (forebrain context)

## 7. Glossary
- **OVB-organoid**: Optic vesicle-bearing organoid
- **Optic vesicle**: Evagination of anterior neuroepithelium that forms the retina
- **Eye field**: Anterior neural plate region fated to form optic vesicles
- **RPE**: Retinal pigment epithelium — non-neural retinal cell type
- **Bilateral symmetry**: Paired left-right structure; fundamental feature of vertebrate eye development
