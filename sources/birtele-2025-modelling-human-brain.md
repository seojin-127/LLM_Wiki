## One-line Summary
Comprehensive roadmap review of brain organoid technology: features, advantages/limitations vs. other model systems, bioengineering improvements, high-throughput analyses, and future applications for studying neocortex development, evolution, and neurodevelopmental disorders.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Nature Reviews Molecular Cell Biology
- Published: 2025 (published online December 2024)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Systematic comparison of brain organoids vs. 2D culture, animal models (mouse, NHP) across key parameters
- Covers neocortex-focused organoid development: progenitor biology, cortical layers, OSVZ
- Reviews bioengineering enhancements: vascularization (Mansour 2018, Cakir 2019), assembloids, microfluidics
- High-throughput analyses: organoid + scRNA-seq + CRISPR screens (CHOOSE, Li 2023)
- Ethical considerations of advanced brain organoids
- Future applications: patient-specific disease modeling, drug screening, evolutionary studies

## 3. Methods & Architecture
- Roadmap review article; comprehensive synthesis of organoid field up to 2024

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Organoids: superior to 2D for 3D architecture, cell type diversity; inferior to in vivo for vasculature, immune, circuit maturity
- Key technologies: assembloids (Pasca lab), ALI-COs (Lancaster 2019), in vivo transplantation (Mansour 2018)
- Limitations: necrotic core, batch variability, limited circuit maturation, ethical concerns

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
- Review article; no original data
- Field advancing rapidly; review represents state-of-art as of late 2024

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013: original cerebral organoid paper
- Pasca et al. 2015: cortical spheroids
- Mansour et al. 2018: in vivo vascularization
- Cakir et al. 2019: in vitro vascularization

## 7. Glossary
- **Assembloid**: Fused organoids of different brain regions for circuit modeling
- **ALI-CO**: Air-liquid interface cerebral organoid — improved survival and axon growth
- **OSVZ**: Outer subventricular zone — expanded progenitor zone; human brain expansion
