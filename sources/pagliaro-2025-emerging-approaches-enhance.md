## One-line Summary
Review of emerging approaches to improve brain organoid physiological relevance: stem cell optimization, morphogen strategies, ECM manipulation, assembloids, co-cultures, and in vivo xenotransplantation.

## 1. Document Info
- Journal/Conference: Trends in Cell Biology
- Published: June 2025 (Vol. 35, No. 6)

## 2. Key Contributions
- Reviews strategies to improve brain organoid quality, reproducibility, and in vivo resemblance
- Covers: stem cell optimization, morphogen administration, ECM niche alteration, tissue architecture manipulation
- Discusses assembloids (fused organoids), co-cultures, and in vivo xenotransplantation for cell diversity
- Speculates on future paths to more physiologically advanced brain organoids

## 3. Methods & Architecture
Review article.

## 4. Key Results & Benchmarks
- Assembloids: fusing region-specific organoids improves cellular diversity and circuit formation
- In vivo xenotransplantation: provides vascularization and maturation signals
- ECM and morphogen optimization: improves lamination and reproducibility

## 5. Limitations & Future Work
Review limitations; field still evolving rapidly.

## 6. Related Work
- Lancaster et al. 2013; Pasca et al. 2015; Birey et al. 2017 (assembloids)

## 7. Glossary
- **Assembloid**: Fusion of two or more region-specific organoids to study connectivity
- **ECM**: Extracellular matrix — scaffold for organoid architecture
- **Xenotransplantation**: Transplanting human organoids into animal hosts for vascularization/maturation
